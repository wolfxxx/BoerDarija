Module 8: Present Tense (`ka-` verbs)

Learn to talk about regular actions and habits using the `ka-` prefix with common verbs.

The `ka-` Prefix for Present Tense

In Darija, to talk about actions that happen regularly, habitually, or are generally true in the present, you often add the prefix `ka-` (كا) to the verb stem. This is different from the verb "to want" (`bghit`) or "to have" (`3ndi`) which have their own special forms.

The verb stem itself also changes slightly depending on the pronoun (who is doing the action).

Pattern: **`ka-` + [Pronoun Prefix] + [Verb Root] + [Pronoun Suffix (sometimes)]**

This might seem complex, so let's look at examples. The key is that the `ka-` part stays consistent for this tense.

Conjugating Common Verbs with `ka-`

Verb: "to eat" - Root: `kul` (كول)

Pronoun Darija (Script) Transcription Meaning Audio
Ana (I)كاناكلkanakulI eat
Nta (You m.s.)كاتاكلkatakulYou eat
Nti (You f.s.)كاتاكليkatakliYou eat
Huwa (He)كاياكلkayakulHe eats
Hiya (She)كاتاكلkatakulShe eats
7na (We)كاناكلوkanakluWe eat
Ntuma (You pl.)كاتاكلوkatakluYou (pl) eat
Huma (They)كاياكلوkayakluThey eat
Observe the Patterns:
  • `ka-` is always there.
  • `Ana` often starts with `n-` after `ka-`.
  • `Nta/Nti/Hiya/Ntuma` often start with `t-` after `ka-`.
  • `Huwa/Huma` often start with `y-` after `ka-`.
  • Feminine singular `Nti` often ends in `-i`.
  • Plural `7na/Ntuma/Huma` often end in `-u`.
These are general tendencies; each verb group can have variations.

NtiكتمشيkatmshiYou (f.s) go

For verbs ending in "-a" like `msha` (to go) or `qra` (to read), the `Nti` form doesn't always add an extra `-i` as it's already there in the stem `mshi`.

Example Sentences

English Sentence Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I eat bread every day. كاناكل الخبز كل نهار. Kanakul l-khobz kul nhar.
She drinks tea in the morning. كاتشرب أتاي فالصباح. Katshrb atay f s-sba7.
They go to school. كيمشيو للمدرسة. Kayimshiw l l-mdrasa.
What do you (m.s.) see? (to see: shaf, kayshuf) شنو كاتشوف؟ Shnu katshuf?
