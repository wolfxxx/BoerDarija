Module 17: Expressing Likes/Dislikes (`kay3jbni...`)

Learn the common Darija structure for saying you like or dislike something, which literally means "it pleases me."

The Structure: "It pleases me"

Instead of a direct verb like "to like," Darija commonly uses the verb `3jb` (عجب) which means "to please." The thing being liked is the grammatical subject, and the person doing the liking is the grammatical object (indicated by a suffix).

The basic structure is: **[Verb Form based on Liked Item] + [Object Suffix for Liker]**

Verb Forms (Based on the Liked Item):

  • If the item liked is **Masculine Singular**: Use `kay3jb-` (كايعجب)
  • If the item liked is **Feminine Singular**: Use `kat3jb-` (كاتعجب)
  • If the items liked are **Plural**: Use `kay3jb-u-` (كايعجبو)

Object Suffixes (The Person Liking):

SuffixMeaning (Liked By...)
-niMe
-ekYou (m.s. / f.s.)
-uHim
-haHer
-naUs
-kumYou (plural)
-humThem

Putting it Together: Examples

Let's combine the verb form and the suffix.

English Sentence Breakdown (Verb + Suffix + Item) Full Darija Sentence (Script) Full Darija Sentence (Transcription) Audio
I like the book (Book=M.S.). `kay3jb-` + `-ni` + `l-ktab` كايعجبني الكتاب. Kay3jbni l-ktab.
I like the city (City=F.S.). `kat3jb-` + `-ni` + `l-mdina` كاتعجبني المدينة. Kat3jbni l-mdina.
I like the books (Books=Plural). `kay3jb-u-` + `-ni` + `l-ktub` كايعجبوني الكتوب. Kay3jbuni l-ktub.
You (m/f) like tea (Tea=M.S.). `kay3jb-` + `-ek` + `atay` كايعجبك أتاي. Kay3jbek atay.
He likes the car (Car=F.S.). `kat3jb-` + `-u` + `t-tomobil` كاتعجبو الطموبيل. Kat3jbu t-tomobil.
She likes couscous (Couscous=M.S.). `kay3jb-` + `-ha` + `ksksu` كايعجبها كسكسو. Kay3jbha ksksu.
We like the children (Children=Plural). `kay3jb-u-` + `-na` + `l-wlad` كايعجبونا الولاد. Kay3jbuna l-wlad.
Key takeaway: The verb changes based on what is liked (M/F/Pl), and the suffix changes based on who likes it.

Liking Activities (Verbs)

To say you like *doing* something (liking a verb), you often use the structure:
**[kay3jb-/kat3jb-/kay3jbu-] + [Suffix] + `n-` + [Verb Stem]**

The `n-` prefix often marks the infinitive-like form used after verbs like "to like" or "to want".

English Sentence Darija Script Transcription Audio
I like to drink tea. كايعجبني نشرب أتاي. Kay3jbni nshrb atay.
She likes to read books. كايعجبها تقرا الكتوب. Kay3jbha tqra l-ktub.
Do you (m.s.) like to go to the market? واش كايعجبك تمشي للسوق؟ Wash kay3jbek tmshi l s-souq?
Simplification: Sometimes people might just say `3ziz 3liya...` (عزيز عليا - dear to me) as another way to express liking, especially strong preference. E.g., `3ziz 3liya atay` (Tea is dear to me / I really like tea). But `kay3jbni` is very common and versatile.

Expressing Dislike

To say you *don't* like something, simply negate the `kay3jb-` structure using `ma-...-sh`.

Pattern: **`ma` + [kay3jb-/kat3jb-/kay3jbu-] + [Suffix] + `sh` + [Liked Item]**

English Sentence Darija Script Transcription Audio
I don't like coffee (Coffee=F.S.). ما كاتعجبنيش القهوة. Ma kat3jbnish l-qhwa.
He doesn't like the film (Film=M.S.). ما كايعجبوش الفيلم. Ma kay3jbush l-film.
We don't like these cars (Cars=Plural). ما كايعجبوناش هاد الطموبيلات. Ma kay3jbunash had t-tomobilat.
I don't like to go out at night. ما كايعجبنيش نخرج بالليل. Ma kay3jbnish nkhruj b l-lil.

Exercise 1: Complete the Sentence

Fill in the blank with the correct form of `kay3jb-` + suffix.

  1. (I like this house - f.s.) Kat3jbni had d-dar.
  2. (He likes mint tea - m.s.) Kay3jbu atay b n-n3na3.
  3. (Do you (f.s.) like these books - plural?) Wash kay3jbuk had l-ktub?
  4. (They don't like the big car - f.s.) Ma kat3jbhumsh t-ttomobil l-kbira.

Exercise 2: Translate

Translate these sentences into Darija:

  1. She likes couscous (m.s.). Kay3jbha ksksu.
  2. I don't like milk (m.s.). Ma kay3jbnish l-7lib.
  3. We like to read (qra). Kay3jbna nqraw.
  4. Do you (plural) like this city (f.s.)? Wash kat3jbkum had l-mdina?
Try talking about things you genuinely like or dislike using this structure. Do you like tea (`atay`)? `Kay3jbni atay.` Do you dislike coffee (`qhwa`)? `Ma kat3jbnish l-qhwa.`
