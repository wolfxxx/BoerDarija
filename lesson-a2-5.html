A2 Module 5: Future Tense with `Ghadi`

Learn how to talk about future plans and intentions using the common future marker `ghadi`.

Introducing the Future Tense

To express future actions in Darija, the most common way is to use the particle `ghadi` (غادي), which means "going to" or "will." It's placed before the main verb.

The main verb that follows `ghadi` is usually in a form similar to its present tense conjugation (often the `ka-` form without the `ka-`, or the simple stem for some persons).

Pattern: **[Subject Pronoun (optional)] + `ghadi` (or variant) + [Main Verb Form]**

Conjugating `ghadi` (or using its variants)

There are a few ways `ghadi` is used:

  1. Invariable `ghadi` or `gha`:** In many casual contexts, especially in Casablanca, `ghadi` or a shortened `gha` can be used for all persons without changing. This is often the easiest for learners.
    Example: Ana `gha` nmshi. (I will go), Nta `gha` tktb. (You will write).
  2. `ghadi` conjugating like an adjective:** `ghadi` (m.s.), `ghada` (f.s.), `ghadin` (pl.). This is also common.

For this A2 module, we will focus on the **invariable `ghadi` / `gha`** combined with the appropriate present tense stem of the main verb (usually the one without the `ka-` prefix, but with the pronoun prefixes like `n-`, `t-`, `y-`).

Pronoun Future Construction (Darija Script) Transcription Meaning (with "to go" - `msha`) Audio
Ana (I) غادي نمشي ghadi nmshi (or `gha nmshi`) I will go
Nta (You m.s.) غادي تمشي ghadi tmshi (or `gha tmshi`) You will go
Nti (You f.s.) غادي تمشي ghadi tmshi (or `gha tmshi`) You will go
Huwa (He) غادي يمشي ghadi ymshi (or `gha ymshi`) He will go
Hiya (She) غادي تمشي ghadi tmshi (or `gha tmshi`) She will go
7na (We) غادي نمشيو ghadi nmshiw (or `gha nmshiw`) We will go
Ntuma (You pl.) غادي تمشيو ghadi tmshiw (or `gha tmshiw`) You (pl) will go
Huma (They) غادي يمشيو ghadi ymshiw (or `gha ymshiw`) They will go
The Main Verb Form: The verb after `ghadi` uses the prefixes `n-` (for Ana), `t-` (for Nta, Nti, Hiya, Ntuma), `y-` (for Huwa, Huma), and the plural suffix `-u` (for 7na, Ntuma, Huma). This is similar to the present `ka-` tense, just without the `ka-`.

More Examples with Other Verbs

English Sentence Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I will eat couscous. (`kul` - to eat) غادي ناكل كسكسو. Ghadi nakul ksksu.
She will drink tea. (`shrb` - to drink) غادي تشرب أتاي. Ghadi tshrb atay.
He will write a letter. (`kteb` - to write) غادي يكتب برا. Ghadi ykteb bra.
We will see a film. (`shaf` - to see) غادي نشوفو فيلم. Ghadi nshufu film.
They will do their homework. (`dar` - to do; `tamarin` - homework) غادي يديرو التمارين ديالهم. Ghadi ydiru t-tamarin dyalhum.

To specify when in the future an action will happen:

Example Sentences with Time Expressions:

  • I will go to the market tomorrow: غادي نمشي للسوق غدا. (Ghadi nmshi l s-souq ghdda.)
  • She will travel next week: غادي تسافر السيمانة الجاية. (Ghadi tsafr s-simana j-jaya.) (`tsafr` from `safr` - to travel)

Negating the Future Tense

To say "I will not..." or "I am not going to...", you negate `ghadi` using `ma-...-sh`.

Pattern: **`ma ghadi-sh` + [Main Verb Form]**

(The `ghadi` part itself is negated. The `-sh` attaches to `ghadi`.)

English Sentence Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I will not go. ما غاديش نمشي. Ma ghadi-sh nmshi. (or `ma gha-sh nmshi`)
He will not eat. ما غاديش ياكل. Ma ghadi-sh yakul.
They will not travel tomorrow. ما غاديش يسافرو غدا. Ma ghadi-sh ysafru ghdda.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Form Future Sentences

Write the future tense for the following using `ghadi` + verb:

  1. Ana (to drink - `shrb`): Ghadi nshrb.
  2. Huwa (to write - `kteb`): Ghadi ykteb.
  3. 7na (to see - `shaf`): Ghadi nshufu.
  4. Hiya (not to go - `msha`): Ma ghadi-sh tmshi.

Exercise 2: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences about future plans:

  1. We will travel next month. Ghadi nsafru sh-shher j-jay.
  2. I will not eat tagine tomorrow. Ma ghadi-sh nakul ttajin ghdda.
  3. What will you (m.s.) do later? Shnu ghadi tdir mn ba3d?
  4. She will visit her family soon. Ghadi tzur l-3a'ila dyalha qrib. (`tzur` from `zar` - to visit)
Talking about the future is exciting! Try to make sentences about your own plans for tomorrow or next week.
