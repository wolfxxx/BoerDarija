Module 12: Prepositions of Place & Basic Conjunctions

Learn how to describe where things are and connect simple ideas using essential prepositions and conjunctions.

Prepositions of Place: Where is it?

These words help you specify the location of objects or people relative to each other.

Preposition (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Sentence (Darija) Example Sentence (Transcription) English Translation Audio
ف / f in, at الستيلو ف الصاك. S-stilo f s-sak. The pen is in the bag.
على / 3la on, upon الكتاب على الطبلة. L-ktab 3la t-tbla. The book is on the table.
تحت / t7t under الكرة تحت الكرسي. L-kura t7t l-kursi. The ball is under the chair.
حدا / 7da next to, beside, near الدار حدا الجامع. D-dar 7da j-jam3. The house is next to the mosque.
قدام / qddam in front of الطموبيل قدام الباب. T-ttomobil qddam l-bab. The car is in front of the door.
مورا / mura behind الشجرة مورا الدار. Sh-shjra mura d-dar. The tree is behind the house.
بين / bin between الصاك بين الكتاب و الستيلو. S-sak bin l-ktab u s-stilo. The bag is between the book and the pen.
Remember `l-` (The): Notice how nouns often take the definite article `l-` (or its variations like `s-`, `t-`, `d-`, `j-` etc.) when used with prepositions of place, as you're usually talking about a specific object's location.

Basic Conjunctions: Connecting Ideas

These are essential for linking words or simple phrases.

Conjunction (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Sentence (Darija) Example Sentence (Transcription) English Translation Audio
و / u / w and أنا و نتا. Ana u nta. Me and you.
و / u / w and كاناكل الخبز و الفرماج. Kanakul l-khobz u l-frmaj. I eat bread and cheese.
أو / ولا / aw / wella or أتاي ولا قهوة؟ Atay wella qhwa? Tea or coffee?
أو / ولا / aw / wella or نمشيو للسوق ولا للدار؟ Nmshiw l s-souq wella l d-dar? Shall we go to the market or to the house?
`wella` vs `aw`: `wella` is very common in spoken Darija for "or." `aw` is closer to formal Arabic but also sometimes heard.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Where Is It?

Choose the best preposition to complete the sentence based on the English meaning.

  1. L-ktab 3la t-tbla. (The book is ON the table.)
  2. Ana kanskn (I live) f Casablanca. (I live IN Casablanca.)
  3. L-kursi qddam l-biru (desk). (The chair is IN FRONT OF the desk.)
  4. Hiya kayna (she is) bin Youssef u Fatima. (She is BETWEEN Youssef AND Fatima.)
  5. T-tilifun t7t l-ktab. (The phone is UNDER the book.)
  6. Bghit atay bla skkar. (I want tea WITHOUT sugar.)

Exercise 2: Translate

Translate these simple phrases/sentences into Darija:

  1. Bread and water. L-khobz u l-ma.
  2. The car is behind the house. T-ttomobil mura d-dar.
  3. Tea or coffee? Atay wella qhwa?
  4. The key is next to the door. S-sarut 7da l-bab.
Describing locations and connecting simple ideas are fundamental skills. Try describing where things are in your room using these prepositions!
