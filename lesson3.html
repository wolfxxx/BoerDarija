Module 3: The Verb "To Have" & Expressing Possession

Learn how to say "I have," "you have," etc., and how to show ownership of things in Darija.

The Verb "To Have": `3nd-` + Suffixes

In Darija, "to have" is expressed using the root `3nd-` (عْند) combined with pronoun suffixes. This is a very common and essential structure.

English Darija Script Transcription Breakdown Audio
I have عندي 3ndi `3nd-` + `-i` (my)
You have (masc. sing.) عندك 3ndek `3nd-` + `-ek` (your)
You have (fem. sing.) عندك 3ndek `3nd-` + `-ek` (your)
He has عندو 3ndu `3nd-` + `-u` (his)
She has عندها 3ndha `3nd-` + `-ha` (her)
We have عندنا 3ndna `3nd-` + `-na` (our)
You have (plural) عندكم 3ndkum `3nd-` + `-kum` (your pl.)
They have عندهُم 3ndhum `3nd-` + `-hum` (their)
Consistency is Key: Notice how the suffixes (`-i`, `-ek`, `-u`, `-ha`, `-na`, `-kum`, `-hum`) are the same ones used for direct possessive suffixes on nouns (which we'll see next) and object pronouns.

Examples with `3nd-`

English Sentence Darija Script Transcription Audio
I have a book. عندي كتاب. 3ndi ktab.
She has a car. عندها طموبيل. 3ndha ttomobil.
Do you (m.s.) have money? واش عندك الفلوس؟ Wash 3ndek l-flus?

Possession Method 1: Direct Suffixes on Nouns

One way to show possession ("my book," "your house") is by adding possessive suffixes directly to the noun. This is very common for close relationships (like family) and body parts, but can be used for other things too.

The suffixes are the same as those used with `3nd-`.

Example: "Book" (كتاب - ktab)

English Darija Script Transcription Breakdown Audio
My book كتابي ktab-i `ktab` + `-i`
Your (m.s.) book كتابك ktab-ek `ktab` + `-ek`
His book كتابو ktab-u `ktab` + `-u`
Her book كتابها ktab-ha `ktab` + `-ha`
Our book كتابنا ktab-na `ktab` + `-na`
Your (pl.) book كتابكم ktab-kum `ktab` + `-kum`
Their book كتابهُم ktab-hum `ktab` + `-hum`
Feminine Nouns ending in -a: If a feminine noun ends in "-a" (like مدرسة - mdrasa, school), the "-a" often changes to "-t-" before adding the possessive suffix. For example, "my school" becomes مدرستي (mdras-t-i).
Example: طموبيلتي (ttomobil-t-i) - my car.

Possession Method 2: Using `dyal` (of)

Another very common way to show possession is using the word `dyal` (ديال), which means "of" or "belonging to." You place the possessed item first, then `dyal`, then the possessor (either a pronoun suffix attached to `dyal` or a noun).

Structure: **[Possessed Item] + `dyal` + [Possessor Suffix/Noun]**

`dyal` with Possessive Suffixes

English Darija Script Transcription Breakdown Audio
The book of mine (My book) الكتاب ديالي L-ktab dyal-i `L-ktab` + `dyal-` + `-i`
The car of yours (m.s.) (Your car) الطموبيل ديالك T-ttomobil dyal-ek `T-ttomobil` + `dyal-` + `-ek`
The house of his (His house) الدار ديالو D-dar dyal-u `D-dar` + `dyal-` + `-u`
The pen of hers (Her pen) الستيلو ديالها S-stilo dyal-ha `S-stilo` + `dyal-` + `-ha`

`dyal` with Nouns (Names)

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
The book of Fatima (Fatima's book) الكتاب ديال فاطمة L-ktab dyal Fatima
The car of Ahmed (Ahmed's car) الطموبيل ديال أحمد T-ttomobil dyal Ahmed
`dyal` vs. Direct Suffixes: Both methods are correct. `dyal` is very flexible and can be used with almost any noun. Direct suffixes are common but might feel more natural for certain items (like family, body parts, or very common possessions). Sometimes `dyal` can add a slight emphasis or clarity. For A1, it's good to be familiar with both. `Dyal` is often easier for beginners to apply consistently.

Exercise 1: "To Have" Sentences

Translate these sentences into Darija using `3nd-`:

  1. We have a big house. 3ndna dar kbira.
  2. He has a new car. 3ndu ttomobil jdida.
  3. Do they have children? Wash 3ndhum wlad?

Exercise 2: Direct Possessive Suffixes

Add the correct direct possessive suffix:

  • (My father) أب → أبي (ab-i)
  • (Your (f.s.) mother) أم → أمك (umm-ek)
  • (His brother) أخ → أخوه (khu-h / akh-u)
  • (Our school - mdrasa) مدرسة → مدرستنا (mdras-t-na)

Exercise 3: Using `dyal`

Translate these using `dyal`:

  1. My pen (s-stilo). S-stilo dyali.
  2. Their house (d-dar). D-dar dyalhum.
  3. Youssef's phone (t-tilifun). T-tilifun dyal Youssef.
