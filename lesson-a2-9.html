A2 Module 9: Health & Body

Learn vocabulary for parts of the body, common health problems, and how to talk to a doctor or pharmacist.

Common Health Problems & Symptoms

How to say you're not feeling well.

English Symptom/Problem Darija (Script) Transcription Notes Audio
I am sick/ill (m./f.) أنا مريض / مريضة Ana mrid / mrida
My head hurts me. (I have a headache) كايضرني راسي. KayDerrni rasi. `Derr` = to hurt + object suffix
My stomach hurts me. كاتضرني كرشي. KatDerrni kershi. `kershi` is feminine, so `katDerrni`
I have a fever. فيا السخانة. Fiya s-skhana. Lit. "In me is the fever."
I have a cold/flu. ضاربني البرد. / فيا الرواح. Darbni l-berd. / Fiya r-rwa7. `Darbni l-berd` lit. "The cold hit me."
I have a cough. فيا الكحة. Fiya l-ke77a.
I feel dizzy. فيا الدوخة. Fiya d-dokha.
I am tired (m./f.) أنا عيان / عيانة Ana 3iyyan / 3iyyana
`KayDerrni / KatDerrni` (It hurts me): The verb `Derr` (ضر - to hurt) is conjugated based on what is hurting (masculine `kayDerr-`, feminine `katDerr-`) and then takes an object suffix for the person feeling the pain (`-ni` for me, `-ek` for you, etc.).
`Fiya` (In me / I have): Used for many symptoms like fever, cold, cough, dizziness.

At the Doctor's (`3nd t-tbib`) / Pharmacy (`f l-farmasiyan`)

English Phrase Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I need a doctor. بغيت طبيب. / خاصني طبيب. Bghit tbib. / Khassni tbib.
I have an appointment. عندي موعد. 3ndi maw3id.
What's wrong with you? (m./f.) (What do you have?) مالك؟ / شنو عندك؟ Malek? / Shnu 3ndek?
I need medicine. بغيت الدوا. / خاصني الدوا. Bghit d-dwa. / Khassni d-dwa.
Medicine for... الدوا ديال... D-dwa dyal...
Get well soon! (To m./f.) الله يشافيك. Allah yshafik.

Dialogue: At the Pharmacy

Patient: السلام عليكم.
Ssalām 3likum.
Hello.

Pharmacist: و عليكم السلام. مالك؟
Wa 3likum ssalam. Malek?
Hello. What's wrong with you?

Patient: كايضرني راسي و فيا السخانة شوية.
KayDerrni rasi u fiya s-skhana shwiya.
My head hurts and I have a bit of a fever.

Pharmacist: واخا. خود هاد الدوا. شرَب حبة وحدة تلاتة د المرات ف النهار.
Wakha. Khud had d-dwa. Shrb 7ebba we7da tlata d l-mrrat f n-nhar.
Okay. Take this medicine. Drink one pill three times a day.

Patient: شكراً بزاف. بشحال؟
Shukran bzzaf. Bsh7al?
Thank you very much. How much?

Pharmacist: عشرين درهم. الله يشافيك.
3shrin derhem. Allah yshafik.
20 dirhams. Get well soon.

Dialogue Vocab:
  • `Khud` (خود): Take (command to m.s.)
  • `7ebba we7da` (حبة وحدة): One pill
  • `tlata d l-mrrat` (تلاتة د المرات): Three times
  • `f n-nhar` (ف النهار): In the day / per day

Exercise 2: Symptoms

How would you say the following in Darija?

  1. My back hurts me. KayDerrni Dheri.
  2. She has a cough. Fiha l-ke77a.
  3. Are you (f.s.) tired? Wash nti 3iyyana?
  4. I don't have a fever. Ma fiya-sh s-skhana.

Exercise 3: At the Doctor

You go to the doctor. The doctor asks: `Malek?` (What's wrong?). How do you reply if your stomach hurts and you feel dizzy?

Reply: KatDerrni kershi u fiya d-dokha.

Being able to describe basic health issues is very useful. Try to make sentences about how you feel today, even if you feel good (`Ana mzyan, l-7amdullah!`).
