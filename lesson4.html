Module 4: Numbers (1-100) & Telling Your Age

Learn to count from 1 to 100 in Darija and how to ask and state your age.

Numbers 0-10

Let's start with the very basics. These are fundamental building blocks.

0: صفر (sifr / zero)
1: واحد (wa7ed)
2: جوج (juj)
3: تلاتة (tlata)
4: ربعة (rb3a)
5: خمسة (khmsa)
6: ستة (setta)
7: سبعة (sb3a)
8: تمنية (tmnya)
9: تسعود (ts3ud)
10: عشرة (3shra)
Counting Objects (1 & 2): When counting objects, "one" and "two" can sometimes change form or be used with gendered nouns, but for basic counting, `wa7ed` and `juj` are standard.

Numbers 11-19

These numbers generally end with a "-tash" sound, similar to "-teen" in English.

Combining Numbers (21-99)

To form numbers like 21, 35, etc., you state the unit first, then "u" (and), then the ten.
Pattern: **[Unit (1-9)] + `u` + [Ten (20, 30...)]**

Number Breakdown (Darija) Full Darija Transcription Audio
21 واحد و عشرين واحد وعشرين wa7ed u 3shrin
35 خمسة و تلاتين خمسة وتلاتين khmsa u tlatin
67 سبعة و ستين سبعة وستين sb3a u settin
99 تسعود و تسعين تسعود وتسعين ts3ud u ts3in
The "u" Connector: The "u" (و) means "and". It connects the unit digit to the tens digit.

Asking and Telling Age

To talk about age, you use the structure "`3nd-` (to have) + [number] + `3am` (year/years)."

Key Phrases:

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
How old are you? (to male/female) شحال ف عمرك؟ Sh7al f 3mrek?
I am ... years old. عندي ... عام 3ndi ... 3am.

Examples:

  • I am 25 years old: عندي خمسة وعشرين عام. (3ndi khmsa u 3shrin 3am.)
  • He is 40 years old: عندو ربعين عام. (3ndu rb3in 3am.)
  • She is 18 years old: عندها تمنطاش عام. (3ndha tmntash 3am.)
`3am` (year): The word عام (3am) means "year." For ages 3-10, you might sometimes hear سنين (snin - years), e.g., عندو تلاتة سنين (3ndu tlata snin - He is 3 years old). However, using `3am` is generally acceptable and simpler for A1. For ages 11 and above, `3am` is standard. For age 1, it's `3am`, for age 2, it's `3amayn`.
