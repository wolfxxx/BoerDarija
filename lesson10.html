Module 10: Days, Months & Time

Learn to navigate the calendar and the clock in Darija, discussing days, months, and specific times.

Days of the Week

The week in Morocco typically starts on Monday.

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
Mondayالتنينt-tnin
Tuesdayالتلاتt-tlat
Wednesdayالأربعl-arb3
Thursdayالخميسl-khmis
Fridayالجمعةj-jem3a
Saturdayالسبتs-sebt
Sundayالحدl-7edd
Usage: To say "on Monday," you often use `nhar t-tnin` (نهار التنين - day Monday) or simply `t-tnin`. The definite article `l-/t-/j-/s-` is usually included when referring to a specific day.

Telling Time

Time is expressed using `s-sa3a` (الساعة - the hour) followed by the number.

Basic Time Expressions:

Time Darija Script Transcription Audio
1:00الوحدة(s-sa3a) l-we7da
2:00الجوج(s-sa3a) j-juj
3:00التلاتة(s-sa3a) t-tlata
4:00الربعة(s-sa3a) r-rb3a
5:00الخمسة(s-sa3a) l-khmsa
6:00الستة(s-sa3a) s-setta
12:00الطناش(s-sa3a) t-tnash
_ :15 (Quarter past)... و ربع... u rbe3
_ :30 (Half past)... و نص... u nus
_ :45 (Quarter to)... إلا ربع / ... قل ربع... illa rbe3 / ... qell rbe3
`l-we7da` (One): Note that "one o'clock" uses the feminine form `l-we7da`. For other hours, use the standard number names (often with `l-` prefix).

Examples:

  • 3:15 → التلاتة و ربع (t-tlata u rbe3)
  • 7:30 → السبعة و نص (s-sb3a u nus)
  • 1:45 → الجوج إلا ربع (j-juj illa rbe3) - Literally "Two except a quarter"
  • 5 minutes past 4 → الربعة و خمسة دقايق (r-rb3a u khmsa dqayeq)
  • 10 minutes to 9 → التسعود قل عشرة دقايق (t-ts3ud qell 3shra dqayeq) (dqayeq = minutes)
AM/PM: To specify morning or evening, you can add:
  • `dyal s-sba7` (ديال الصباح) - of the morning (AM)
  • `dyal l-3shiya` (ديال العشية) - of the afternoon/evening (PM)
  • `dyal l-lil` (ديال الليل) - of the night (late PM)
Example: 9:00 AM → التسعود ديال الصباح (t-ts3ud dyal s-sba7)

Combining Expressions:

  • This week: `had s-simana`
  • Next month: `sh-shher j-jay` (the coming month)
  • Last year: `l-3am lli fat` (the year that passed)

Exercise 3: Complete the Sentence

Use the time expressions.

  1. I will go tomorrow: Ana ghadi nmshi ghdda.
  2. We drink coffee in the morning: 7na kanshrbu l-qhwa f s-sba7.
  3. He was here yesterday: Huwa kan hna l-bare7.
  4. What time is it now? Sh7al f s-sa3a daba?
Real-World Practice: Try asking a friend (or yourself!) "Sh7al hadi f s-sa3a?" throughout the day and answering in Darija.
