Module 9: Asking Basic Questions

Learn how to ask simple Yes/No questions and use basic question words like Who, What, Where, When, and How Much.

Asking Yes/No Questions with `Wash` (واش)

The easiest way to turn a statement into a Yes/No question in Darija is often by adding the particle `Wash` at the beginning. It works similarly to how English inverts subject-verb order or uses "Do/Does/Are/Is."

Pattern: **`Wash` + [Statement]?**

Statement (Darija) Statement (English) Yes/No Question (Darija) Yes/No Question (English) Audio (Question)
هادا كتاب. (hada ktab) This is a book. واش هادا كتاب؟ Is this a book?
نتا مزيان. (nta mzyan) You (m.s.) are fine. واش نتا مزيان؟ Are you (m.s.) fine?
عندك الفلوس. (3ndek l-flus) You have money. واش عندك الفلوس؟ Do you have money?
كاتشرب أتاي. (katshrb atay) She drinks tea. واش كاتشرب أتاي؟ Does she drink tea?

Answering Yes/No Questions

Answer Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
Yes إييه / آه Iyyeh / Ah
No لا La

Example: واش هادا كتاب؟ إييه، هادا كتاب. (Wash hada ktab? Iyyeh, hada ktab.) - Is this a book? Yes, this is a book.

Example: واش عندك الفلوس؟ لا، ما عنديش. (Wash 3ndek l-flus? La, ma 3ndish.) - Do you have money? No, I don't have. (You'll learn `ma 3ndish` fully in the negation module).

Intonation: While `Wash` is common, sometimes questions are formed simply by using rising intonation at the end of a statement, just like in English. E.g., هادا كتاب؟ (Hada ktab?) with a rising tone can also mean "Is this a book?". Using `Wash` is clearer for learners.

Basic Question Words (Interrogatives)

To ask for specific information (not just Yes/No), you use question words. These usually come at the beginning of the sentence.

Question Word (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Question (Darija) Example Question (Transcription) English Translation Audio (Word/Q)
شنو؟ / Shnu? What? شنو هادا؟ Shnu hada? What is this?
شكون؟ / Shkun? Who? شكون نتا؟ Shkun nta? Who are you? (to m.s.)
فين؟ / Fin? Where? فين الدار؟ Fin d-dar? Where is the house?
إيمتى؟ / Imta? When? إيمتى كتمشي؟ Imta katmshi? When do you (she/f.s.) go?
بشحال؟ / Bsh7al? How much? (Cost) بشحال هاد الكتاب؟ Bsh7al had l-ktab? How much is this book?
شحال؟ / Sh7al? How much? / How many? (Quantity) شحال عندك ديال لفلوس؟ Sh7al 3ndek dyal l-flus? How much money do you have?
`Sh7al` vs `Bsh7al`: Use `Bsh7al` specifically when asking for the price. Use `Sh7al` for general quantity ("how much/how many") or age (`Sh7al f 3mrek?`).

Exercise 1: Yes/No Questions

Turn these statements into Yes/No questions using `Wash`.

  1. هي طبيبة. (Hiya tbiba.) → Wash hiya tbiba?
  2. عندهم سيارة. (3ndhum siyara.) → Wash 3ndhum siyara?
  3. هاداك هو السوق. (Hadak huwa s-souq.) → Wash hadak huwa s-souq?

Exercise 2: Choose the Right Question Word

Fill in the blank with the correct question word (Shnu, Shkun, Fin, Imta, Bsh7al, Sh7al).

  1. Fin l-7anut? (Where is the shop?)
  2. Shkun had l-mra? (Who is this woman?)
  3. Bsh7al l-khobz? (How much is the bread?)
  4. Shnu bghiti takul? (What do you want to eat?)
  5. Imta kayji l- ट्रेन (train)? (When does the train come?)
  6. Sh7al dyal l-wlad 3ndek? (How many children do you have?)

Exercise 3: Form Questions

Translate these questions into Darija:

  1. What is your (m.s.) name? Shnu smitek?
  2. Where is the coffee? Fin l-qhwa?
  3. How much is this (f.)? Bsh7al hadi?
  4. Is this your (m.s.) book? Wash hada ktabek? / Wash hada l-ktab dyalek?
