B1 Module 9: Derived Verb Forms II (Reflexive/Passive - Forms V, VII)

Continue exploring Darija verb patterns, focusing on Form V (`tfe33el`) and Form VII (`nfe3el`) which often indicate reflexive or passive meanings.

Review: Derived Verb Forms & Form II

In the previous module, you were introduced to derived verb forms (`l-awzan`) and specifically Form II (`fe33el`), which often adds a causative or intensive meaning to a basic verb.

Example: `fhhem` (فهّــم - to make understand/explain) from `fhem` (فهم - to understand).

Now, let's look at other important derived forms.

Form V: The `tfe33el` (تفعّــل) Pattern

Form V is characterized by a `t-` prefix added to the Form II pattern. It often has one of these meanings:

  • **Reflexive of Form II:** The subject does the action of Form II to itself (e.g., if Form II is "to teach," Form V might be "to learn" - to teach oneself).
  • **Passive of Form II:** The subject undergoes the action of Form II.
  • It can also indicate a change of state or becoming something.

Examples of Form V Verbs:

Related Form II (Meaning) Form V Verb (Script/Trans.) Meaning of Form V Example Sentence (Form V) Audio (Form V)
علّم (3llem) - to teach تعلّم / t3llem Reflexive: To learn (to teach oneself) كانبغي نتعلّم لغات جداد. (Kanbghi nt3llem lughat jdad.) - I like to learn new languages.
بدّل (beddel) - to change (something) تبدّل / tbeddel Reflexive/Passive: To change (oneself), to be changed الجو تبدّل اليوم. (L-jeww tbeddel l-yum.) - The weather changed today.
صوّر (Suwwer) - to photograph/film تصوّر / tsuwwer Reflexive: To imagine, to take a photo of oneself (less common for this meaning) / Passive: To be photographed تصوّرت بلي أنا ف مكان آخر. (Tsuwwert belli ana f makan akher.) - I imagined that I was in another place.
كلّم (kellem) - to speak to تكلّم / tkellem To speak, to talk خاصك تتكلّم معاه. (Khassk ttkellem m3ah.) - You need to speak with him.
Form V verbs conjugate regularly in the past, present (`ka-`), and future (`ghadi`), following the patterns for verbs starting with `t-`.
Example (Present `ka-` for `t3llem`): Ana `kant3llem`, Nta `katt3llem`, Huwa `kayt3llem`.

Form VII: The `nfe3el` ( انفعل) Pattern

Form VII is characterized by an `n-` prefix (or `en-`, `in-`) added to the Form I root. It often imparts a **passive** or **reflexive-passive** meaning, indicating that the subject undergoes an action or changes state without an explicit doer.

Examples of Form VII Verbs:

Related Form I (Meaning) Form VII Verb (Script/Trans.) Meaning of Form VII Example Sentence (Form VII) Audio (Form VII)
فتح (fte7) - to open (something) انفتح / تحلّ / nfeta7 / t7ell Passive: To be opened, to open (by itself) الباب تحلّ بوحدو. (L-bab t7ell bu7du.) - The door opened by itself. (`t7ell` is more common than `nfeta7` in Darija for "to be opened")
هرس (hres) - to break (something) تهرّس / therres Passive: To be broken, to get broken الكاس تهرّس. (L-kas therres.) - The glass broke / got broken.
كتب (kteb) - to write انكتب / تكتب / nkateb / tkteb Passive: To be written الكتاب تكتب ف 2020. (L-ktab tkteb f 2020.) - The book was written in 2020. (`tkteb` is more common)
حبس (7bes) - to stop/imprison انحبس / تحبس / n7abes / t7bes Passive: To be stopped/imprisoned اللص تحبس. (L-less t7bes.) - The thief was imprisoned. (`L-less` = thief)
Darija Variations: While standard Arabic has a clear Form VII (`nfe3el`), in Darija, the `t-` prefix (similar to Form V or a `t-` prefixed Form I) is often used for passive meanings for some verbs, sometimes more commonly than the `n-` prefix. For example, `t7ell` (to be opened) is very common. `Therres` (to be broken) looks like a Form V derived from Form II (`herres` - to smash).
Focus on recognizing the passive meaning.

Identifying and Using These Forms

  • **Form V (`tfe33el`):** Look for the `t-` prefix combined with a doubled middle consonant. Think "reflexive of Form II" or "to become/undergo the action of Form II."
  • **Form VII (`nfe3el` / `tfe3el` variants):** Look for an `n-` prefix or a `t-` prefix on a Form I root, often indicating something happens *to* the subject or the subject changes state.

These forms conjugate regularly once their pattern is established.

Example: كل نهار كانتعلّم شي حاجة جديدة. (Kul nhar kant3llem shi 7aja jdida.) - Every day I learn something new.

Example: مللي تهرس الكاس، ما بقيتش نقدر نشرب فيه. (Melli therres l-kas, ma bqitsh nqder nshrb fih.) - When the glass broke, I could no longer drink from it.

Derived verb forms unlock a huge amount of vocabulary! Start by recognizing the patterns (`t-` prefix, `n-` prefix, doubled middle consonants) and the typical meaning changes they bring.
