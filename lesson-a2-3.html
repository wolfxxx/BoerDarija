A2 Module 3: Object Pronoun Suffixes

Learn how to use suffixes attached to verbs to indicate "me," "you," "him," "her," "us," and "them" as the object of an action.

What are Object Pronouns?

Subject pronouns (Ana, Nta, Huwa, etc.) are the ones *doing* the action. Object pronouns are the ones *receiving* the action. For example, in "He saw me," "me" is the object pronoun.

In Darija, these object pronouns are usually suffixes attached directly to the end of the verb.

The Object Pronoun Suffixes

These suffixes are very similar to the possessive suffixes you learned for nouns and `3nd-`.

Object Pronoun (English) Suffix (Transcription) Example Verb: `shaf` (to see) Transcription of Example Meaning of Example Audio
me -ni shafني shaf-ni He saw me
you (singular m. & f.) -ek shafك shaf-ek He saw you
him / it (masc.) -u شافو shaf-u He saw him/it
her / it (fem.) -ha شافها shaf-ha He saw her/it
us -na شافنا shaf-na He saw us
you (plural) -kum شافكم shaf-kum He saw you (pl.)
them -hum شافهُم shaf-hum He saw them
Vowel Harmony/Changes: Sometimes, the vowel of the suffix or the end of the verb might slightly adjust for smoother pronunciation. For instance, if a verb ends in a vowel, a linking sound might appear. Listen carefully to the audio examples.
Example with verb ending in `-t` (like "I saw" - `sheft`):
  • I saw him: شفتو (`sheft-u`)
  • I saw her: شفتها (`sheft-ha`)

Examples in Sentences (Past Tense)

Let's see these suffixes in action with past tense verbs you've learned.

English Sentence Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I saw you (m.s.) yesterday. شفتك البارح. Sheftek l-bare7.
She wrote me a letter. كتبات ليا برا. Ketbat li-ya bra.
He helped us. (To help: عاون `3awen`) عاونا. 3awen-na.
Did they understand you (f.s.)? واش فهموك؟ Wash fhem-u-k?
I gave him the book. (To give: عطى `3ta`) عطيتو الكتاب. 3titu l-ktab.
Indirect Objects: For verbs like "to give" (`3ta`) or "to write to" (`kteb l-`), the recipient is often an indirect object. "To me" becomes `liya`, "to you" `lik`, "to him" `lih`, etc. This is slightly different from direct object suffixes but related. We'll explore this more later. For now, focus on direct objects with verbs like "see" or "help."
The example "She wrote me a letter" (`Ketbat liya bra`) uses this indirect object form for "to me".

Using with Present Tense `ka-` Verbs

Object suffixes can also be attached to present tense `ka-` verbs.

English Sentence Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
He sees me every day. كايشوفني كل نهار. Kayishuf-ni kul nhar.
I understand you (f.s.) well. كانفهمك مزيان. Kanfhem-ek mzyan.
Does she help them? واش كاتعاونهم؟ Wash kat3awen-hum?

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Identify the Suffix

What object pronoun does the suffix represent in these Darija verbs?

  1. سمعتك (sma3t-ek): You (s.)
  2. عاونهم (3awen-hum): Them
  3. ضربني (Dreb-ni): Me (Dreb = to hit)
  4. كايعرفنا (Kay3rf-na): Us (Kay3ref = he knows)

Exercise 2: Add the Suffix

Attach the correct object pronoun suffix to the verb.

  1. Ana sheft + (her) → Ana sheft-ha
  2. Huwa kteb + (you s.) → Huwa kteb-ek
  3. Hiya katakul + (it - masc., e.g., bread) → Hiya katakul-u
  4. 7na bghina + (them) → 7na bghina-hum

Exercise 3: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences using object pronoun suffixes:

  1. He helped me yesterday. 3awenni l-bare7.
  2. I see you (plural) now. Kanshufkum daba.
  3. She understood him. Fhemtu. (or Fhemet-u)
  4. Did you (f.s.) call us? (To call: عيّط على `3iyyet 3la`)
    Wash 3iyyetti 3lina? (This uses preposition + suffix)
Object pronouns make your sentences much more dynamic! Notice how they are very similar to possessive suffixes. This pattern repetition is helpful. The example with "call us" shows suffixes on prepositions, which is also very common.
