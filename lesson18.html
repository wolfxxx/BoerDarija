Module 18: A1 Review & Consolidation

Mabrouk! Congratulations on reaching the end of the A1 level. Let's review what you've learned and practice combining everything.

A1 Level Recap

Over the past 17 modules (including Module 0), you've covered the foundations of Moroccan Darija (Casablanca dialect). Here's a quick checklist of the main topics:

  • Darija Sounds & Transcription
  • Greetings & Politeness Formulas
  • Introductions & Subject Pronouns
  • "Zero Copula" (Is/Am/Are)
  • Nouns & Grammatical Gender
  • Definite Article "l-"
  • Verb "To Have" (`3ndi`)
  • Possession (Suffixes & `dyal`)
  • Numbers 1-100 & Telling Age
  • Demonstratives (This/That/These/Those)
  • Adjectives & Agreement
  • Colors
  • Verb "To Want" (`bghit`)
  • Present Tense (`ka-` for habits)
  • Asking Yes/No Questions (`Wash`)
  • Basic Question Words (Who, What, Where, When, How Much?)
  • Days, Months & Telling Time
  • Family Member Vocabulary
  • Prepositions of Place
  • Basic Conjunctions (and, or)
  • Negation (`ma-...-sh`, `mashi`, `ma 3ndish`)
  • Imperatives (Commands)
  • Shopping & Basic Bargaining
  • Locations & Basic Directions
  • Expressing Likes/Dislikes (`kay3jbni`)
If any of these topics feel unfamiliar, now is a great time to go back and review that specific module!

Mixed Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Translation (Darija to English)

Translate the following Darija sentences into English.

  1. هاداك خويا سميتو يوسف، عندو تلاتين عام.
    (Hadak khuya smitu Youssef, 3ndu tlatin 3am.)
    Answer: That is my brother, his name is Youssef, he is 30 years old.
  2. واش كاتعجبك هاد المدينة؟ لا، ما كاتعجبنيش بزاف.
    (Wash kat3jbek had l-mdina? La, ma kat3jbnish bzzaf.)
    Answer: Do you like this city? No, I don't like it very much.
  3. ما بغيتش نمشي للسوق اليوم حيت ماشي مزيان.
    (Ma bghitsh nmshi l s-souq l-yum 7it mashi mzyan.)
    Answer: I don't want to go to the market today because it's not good / I'm not feeling well.
  4. القهوة ديال ماما كاينة على الطبلة حدا الشرجم.
    (L-qhwa dyal mama kayna 3la t-tbla 7da sh-shrjem.)
    Answer: Mom's coffee is on the table next to the window.

Exercise 2: Translation (English to Darija)

Translate the following English sentences into Darija.

  1. I want water without sugar, please.
    Answer: Bghit l-ma bla skkar, 3afak.
  2. Where is the big mosque? Is it far?
    Answer: Fin j-jam3 l-kbir? Wash b3id?
  3. This is my sister's car (use `dyal`).
    Answer: Hadi t-ttomobil dyal khti.
  4. Don't drink that! (to m.s.)
    Answer: Ma tshr bsh hadak!

Exercise 3: Fill-in-the-Blanks

Complete the sentences with an appropriate word/form.

  1. Ana ma kanshrbsh (not drink) atay f l-lil.
  2. D-dar dyali sghira (small - fem.).
  3. Shnu smit (name) had r-rajl?
  4. Kayen ktab t7t (under) l-kursi.
  5. Bsh7al (how much - price) had l-khobz?

Exercise 4: Dialogue Completion

Read the mini-dialogue and fill in Speaker B's responses logically using A1 Darija.

A: السلام عليكم. واش عندك ستيلو زرق؟
(Ssalām 3likum. Wash 3ndek stilo zreq?)

B: و عليكم السلام. Iyyeh, 3ndi. / La, ma 3ndish.
(Wa 3likum ssalam. [Yes, I have. / No, I don't have.])

A: مزيان. بشحال؟
(Mzyan. Bsh7al?)

B: B khmsa drahem. (Example: B khmsa drahem - For 5 dirhams)

A: واخا، بغيت واحد، عافاك.
(Wakha, bghit wa7ed, 3afak.)

B: Wakha. / هاك. (Wakha - Okay. / Hak - Here you go.)

Congratulations & What's Next?

Well done on completing the A1 level modules! You have built a solid foundation in Moroccan Darija, covering essential vocabulary, basic grammar structures, and practical communication skills for everyday situations.

Mastering a language is a journey, and consistency is key. Keep practicing what you've learned:

  • Review the modules regularly.
  • Try to use Darija in simple ways daily (naming objects, simple sentences).
  • Listen to Moroccan music or simple dialogues online.
  • If possible, practice speaking with native speakers, even just basic greetings and questions.

When you feel comfortable with the A1 material, you'll be ready to move on to the A2 level, where you can expect to learn:

  • Past Tense
  • Future Tense
  • More complex sentence structures
  • Object pronouns
  • Expanded vocabulary
  • And much more!
Bslama for now, and good luck with your continued Darija studies!
