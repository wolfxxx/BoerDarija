Module 13: Negation

Learn how to say "not" in Darija, negating verbs, nouns, adjectives, and the verb "to have."

Negating Verbs: `ma-...-sh`

The most common way to negate verbs (like "to eat," "to drink," "to go") is by wrapping them with `ma-` (ما) before the verb and `-sh` (ش) after the verb.

Pattern: **`ma` + [Verb Conjugation] + `sh`**

Affirmative (English) Affirmative (Darija) Negative (Darija Script) Negative (Transcription) Negative (English) Audio (Negative)
I eat kanakul ما كاناكُلش ma kanakulsh I don't eat
She drinks katshrb ما كاتشرَبش ma katshrbsh She doesn't drink
He goes kayimshi ما كايمشيش ma kayimshish He doesn't go
We understand kanfhmu ما كانفهموش ma kanfhmush We don't understand
I want bghit ما بغيتش ma bghitsh I don't want
Pronunciation: The `-sh` is usually attached directly to the verb sound. Sometimes, if the verb ends in a vowel, the `-sh` might sound slightly more distinct. Listen carefully to the audio examples.

Negating Nouns & Adjectives: `mashi`

To say something is "not" a noun or "not" a certain adjective, you use the word `mashi` (ماشي) before the noun or adjective.

Pattern (Noun): **[Subject/Thing] + `mashi` + [Noun]**

Pattern (Adjective): **[Subject/Thing] + `mashi` + [Adjective]**

Affirmative (English) Affirmative (Darija) Negative (Darija Script) Negative (Transcription) Negative (English) Audio (Negative)
This is a book. Hada ktab. هادا ماشي كتاب. Hada mashi ktab. This is not a book.
She is a teacher. Hiya ustada. هي ماشي أستاذة. Hiya mashi ustada. She is not a teacher.
The house is big. D-dar kbira. الدار ماشي كبيرة. D-dar mashi kbira. The house is not big.
He is happy. Huwa fr7an. هو ماشي فرحان. Huwa mashi fr7an. He is not happy.
`mashi` vs. `ma-...-sh`: Remember the difference! Use `ma-...-sh` to negate verbs (actions). Use `mashi` to negate nouns (identity) or adjectives (description).

Negating "To Have": `ma 3nd-...-sh`

To say you "don't have" something, you use the `ma-...-sh` structure around the `3nd-` form (from Module 3).

Pattern: **`ma` + `3nd-`[Suffix] + `sh`**

Affirmative (English) Affirmative (Darija) Negative (Darija Script) Negative (Transcription) Negative (English) Audio (Negative)
I have 3ndi ما عنديش ma 3ndish I don't have
You (m.s.) have 3ndek ما عندكش ma 3ndeksh You don't have
He has 3ndu ما عندوش ma 3ndush He doesn't have
She has 3ndha ما عندهاش ma 3ndhash She doesn't have
We have 3ndna ما عندناش ma 3ndnash We don't have
They have 3ndhum ما عندهُمش ma 3ndhumsh They don't have

Example Sentences:

  • I don't have money: ما عنديش الفلوس. (Ma 3ndish l-flus.)
  • He doesn't have a car: ما عندوش طموبيل. (Ma 3ndush ttomobil.)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Negate the Verb

Change the following sentences to the negative using `ma-...-sh`.

  1. Ana kanshrb atay. → Ana ma kanshrbsh atay.
  2. Huwa kayfhm l-français. → Huwa ma kayfhmsh l-français.
  3. 7na bghina l-ma. → 7na ma bghinash l-ma.

Exercise 2: Negate the Noun/Adjective

Change the following sentences to the negative using `mashi`.

  1. Hada stilo. → Hada mashi stilo.
  2. T-ttomobil qdima. → T-ttomobil mashi qdima.
  3. Nti mrid (sick, f: mrida). → Nti mashi mrida.

Exercise 3: Negate "To Have"

Translate these sentences into Darija:

  1. She doesn't have a book. Ma 3ndhash ktab.
  2. We don't have time (l-weqt). Ma 3ndnash l-weqt.
  3. Do you (m.s.) not have the key? Wash ma 3ndeksh s-sarut?
Being able to say "no" or negate statements is just as important as affirming them. Practice these negative forms frequently!
