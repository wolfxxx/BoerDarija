B1 Module 12: Describing People, Places, and Things in Detail

Combine your knowledge of adjectives, relative clauses, and comparisons to create rich, detailed descriptions.

Review: Key Descriptive Tools

To describe effectively, you'll draw upon several concepts you've learned:

  • **Adjectives & Agreement:** (A1.6, A2.7) - Words that describe nouns, agreeing in gender/number.
  • **Colors:** (A1.6) - A specific type of adjective.
  • **Comparatives & Superlatives:** (A2.8) - `kter mn` (more than), `b7al` (as...as), `a7san` (best).
  • **Relative Clauses with `lli`:** (A2.13, B1.7) - Adding extra information about a noun (who/which/that).
  • **Prepositions of Place:** (A1.12, A2.6) - `f`, `3la`, `7da`, `qddam`, `mura`, etc.
  • **Vocabulary:** For objects, personality, appearance, places.

This module is about practicing how to weave these elements together fluently.

Describing People

When describing people, you might talk about their physical appearance, personality, and what they are doing or wearing.

Key Vocabulary (Review & New):

  • Appearance: `twil` (tall), `qsir` (short), `ghlid` (fat/big), `rqiq` (thin), `zwin` (beautiful/handsome), `khayb` (ugly), `sh3r k7el/twil` (black/long hair), `3inin zrqin/kbar` (blue/big eyes).
  • Personality: `Drif` (kind), `mDe77ek` (funny), `m3qul` (serious), `dki` (smart), `7shumi` (shy), `m3esseb` (angry).
  • Clothing: `qamija` (shirt), `srwal` (pants), `sbbat` (shoes), `jakita` (jacket), `ksuwa` (dress).

Example Description of a Person:

صاحبي أحمد راجل طويل و ضريف. عندو شعر كحل و عينين خضرين. ديما لابس مزيان، غالبا كيلبس قميجة و سروال. هو خدام ف واحد الشركة كبيرة و كايعجبو يقرا الكتوب ف وقت الفراغ ديالو. هو اللي عاوني البارح.
Sa7bi Ahmed rajl twil u Drif. 3ndu sh3r k7el u 3inin khDrin. Dima labes mzyan, ghaliban kaylbes qamija u srwal. Huwa khddam f wa7ed sh-sharika kbira u kay3jbu yqra l-ktub f weqt l-faragh dyalu. Huwa lli 3awenni l-bare7.

Translation: My friend Ahmed is a tall and kind man. He has black hair and green eyes. He's always well-dressed, often he wears a shirt and pants. He works at a big company and he likes to read books in his free time. He is the one who helped me yesterday.

Describing Places

When describing places, focus on size, atmosphere, location, and what you can find or do there.

Key Vocabulary (Review & New):

  • General: `kbir` (big), `sghir` (small), `zwin` (nice/beautiful), `khayb` (ugly/bad), `qdim` (old), `jdid` (new), `nqi` (clean), `mewssekh` (dirty).
  • Atmosphere: `hadئ` (hadi' - calm/quiet), ` فيه الصداع` (fih s-sda3 - noisy/it has noise), `mshmmesh` (sunny), ` مظلم` (mDellm - dark).
  • Features: `shjer` (trees), `b7er` (sea), `jbel` (mountain), `bniyat` (buildings).

Example Description of a Place (e.g., a City):

مراكش مدينة كبيرة و زوينة بزاف. فيها بزاف د الناس و الأسواق التقليدية اللي فيها كلشي. الجو تما كيكون سخون فالصيف ولكن ف الشتا زوين. جامع الفنا هو أشهر بلاصة تما، ديما عامر بالناس.
Mrraksh mdina kbira u zwina bzzaf. Fiha bzzaf d n-nas u l-aswaq t-teqlidiya lli fiha kulshi. L-jeww temma kaykun skhun f s-sif walakin f sh-shta zwin. Jam3 l-Fna huwa ashhar blasa temma, dima 3amr b n-nas.

Translation: Marrakech is a very big and beautiful city. It has a lot of people and traditional markets where you can find everything. The weather there is usually hot in the summer but nice in the winter. Jemaa el-Fna is the most famous place there, always full of people.

Describing Things (Objects)

When describing objects, consider their color, size, shape, material, age, and purpose.

Example Description of an Object (e.g., a Book):

عندي واحد الكتاب قديم و زرق. هو كتاب ديال القصص اللي كايعجبني نقراه قبل ما نعس. الغلاف ديالو مقطع شوية ولكن الصفحات مزال مزيانين. شريتو من واحد السوق صغير.
3ndi wa7ed l-ktab qdim u zreq. Huwa ktab dyal l-qisas lli kay3jbni nqrah qbel ma n3es. L-ghlaf dyalu mqette3 shwiya walakin s-sfa7at mazal mzyanin. Shritu mn wa7ed s-souq sghir.

Translation: I have an old and blue book. It's a book of stories that I like to read before I sleep. Its cover is a bit torn but the pages are still good. I bought it from a small market.

  • `l-ghlaf` (الغلاف) - the cover
  • `mqette3` (مقطع) - torn (passive participle)
  • `s-sfa7at` (الصفحات) - the pages
  • `mazal` (مازال) - still
Layering Descriptions: Start simple: "Hada ktab kbir." (This is a big book.) Add more: "Hada ktab kbir u zreq." (This is a big and blue book.) Add a relative clause: "Hada ktab kbir u zreq lli shritu l-bare7." (This is a big and blue book that I bought yesterday.) Add purpose/opinion: "Hada ktab kbir u zreq lli shritu l-bare7, kay3jbni bzzaf." (...I like it a lot.)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Describe a Family Member

Write 3-4 sentences in Darija describing a family member (e.g., their appearance, personality, something they like to do). Use at least two adjectives and one relative clause (`lli`).

Exercise 2: Describe Your City/Town

Write 3-4 sentences in Darija describing the place where you live. Mention its size, something it's known for, or what you like/dislike about it.

Exercise 3: Describe an Object

Choose an object in your room and describe it in 2-3 Darija sentences. Include its color, size, and maybe what it's used for or where you got it.

The goal of this module is to practice combining different grammatical structures to create richer descriptions. Don't aim for perfection, just try to use the tools you've learned!
