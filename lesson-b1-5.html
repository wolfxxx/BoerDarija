B1 Module 5: Expressing Obligation & Necessity

Learn how to say what you "must" or "need to" do, and what is "necessary" or "obligatory" in Darija.

Expressing Obligation: `khass` (خاص - must / to need)

The most common way to express obligation or need is using `khass` followed by a pronoun suffix (indicating who must/needs) and then usually a verb (often in its present stem form, with `n-/t-/y-` prefixes).

Structure: **`khass` + [Pronoun Suffix] + [Verb]**

Pronoun Suffix Darija Form (e.g., with "to go" - `nmshi`) Transcription Meaning (I must go / I need to go) Audio
-ni (me) خاصني نمشي khassni nmshi I must/need to go
-ek (you s.) خاصك تمشي khassk tmshi You must/need to go
-u (him) خاصو يمشي khassu ymshi He must/needs to go
-ha (her) خاصها تمشي khassha tmshi She must/needs to go
-na (us) خاصنا نمشيو khassna nmshiw We must/need to go
-kum (you pl.) خاصكم تمشيو khasskum tmshiw You (pl) must/need to go
-hum (them) خاصهُم يمشيو khasshum ymshiw They must/need to go

`Khass` can also be used with nouns: خاصني الفلوس. (Khassni l-flus.) - I need money.

Negation: To negate `khass`, you say `ma khassnish...` etc. (e.g., ما خاصنيش نمشي. `Ma khassnish nmshi.` - I don't have to go / I don't need to go.)

Expressing Necessity: `Daruri` (ضروري) & `lazem` (لازم)

`Daruri` means "necessary" or "it is necessary." `Lazem` (from Arabic) also means "it is necessary" or "must" and is often used similarly to `khass`.

Using `Daruri` (Necessary)

Often used with a verb (that usually starts with `n-/t-/y-` based on who should do it) or followed by `3la` + pronoun suffix.

Structure 1: **`Daruri` + [Verb (e.g., `nmshi` - I go/should go)]**

Structure 2: **`Daruri` + `3la` + [Pronoun Suffix] + [Verb]** (It is necessary for [someone] to...)

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
It's necessary to go. / One must go. ضروري نمشيو. / ضروري الواحد يمشي. Daruri nmshiw. / Daruri l-wa7ed ymshi.
It's necessary for me to study. ضروري عليا نقرا. Daruri 3liya nqra.
It's necessary that he comes early. ضروري يجي بكري. Daruri yji bekri.

Using `lazem` (It is necessary / Must)

Often used similarly to `khass`, followed by a verb with the appropriate pronoun prefix.

Structure: **`lazem` + [Verb (e.g., `nmshi` - I go/should go)]** (The implied subject is in the verb)

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I must (it's necessary for me to) sleep. لازم نعس. Lazem n3es.
You (s.) must (it's necessary for you to) eat. لازم تاكل. Lazem takul.
Both `Daruri` and `lazem` express a strong sense of necessity or obligation, often interchangeable with `khass`. `Khass` + pronoun suffix is generally the most common for personal obligation.

Giving Advice: "You should..."

A common way to give advice ("You should...") is by using `khassk` (you must/need to - from above) or by phrasing it as "It's better for you to..."

Using `khassk`:

  • You (m.s.) should rest: خاصك ترتاح. (Khassk trta7.) (`trta7` from `rta7` - to rest)
  • You (f.s.) should see a doctor: خاصك تشوفي طبيب. (Khassk tshufi tbib.)

Using "It's better for you..." - `mn l-a7san lik...` (من الأحسن ليك):

  • It's better for you (m.s.) to sleep early: من الأحسن ليك تنعس بكري. (Mn l-a7san lik tn3es bekri.)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Complete with `khass` + Suffix

Fill in the blanks.

  1. (He needs to work) Khassu ykhdem.
  2. (We need to buy bread) Khassna nshriw l-khobz.
  3. (You (pl.) need to study) Khasskum tqraw.
  4. (She doesn't need to go) Ma khasshash tmshi.

Exercise 2: `Daruri` or `Lazem`

Translate using `Daruri` or `Lazem`.

  1. It's necessary for me to finish this today.
    Answer: Daruri nsali hadshi l-yum. / Lazem nsali hadshi l-yum. (`nsali` - I finish)
  2. It's necessary that they are here at 8.
    Answer: Daruri ykunu hna f t-tmnya. / Lazem ykunu hna f t-tmnya. (`ykunu` - they be)

Exercise 3: Giving Advice

How would you advise someone (m.s.) in these situations?

  1. Your friend is very tired. (Advise: rest)
    Answer: Khassk trta7. / Mn l-a7san lik trta7.
  2. Your friend has an exam tomorrow. (Advise: study)
    Answer: Khassk tqra. / Lazem tqra.
Expressing obligation, necessity, and giving advice are key functions for intermediate conversations. Try to think of situations where you'd use these phrases!
