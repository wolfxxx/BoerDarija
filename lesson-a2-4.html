A2 Module 4: Talking About Past Events & Simple Narratives

Learn how to sequence past actions and tell simple stories about what happened.

Recap: Past Tense & Object Pronouns

In the previous modules, you learned:

  • How to form the past tense for regular and common irregular verbs (e.g., `ktebt` - I wrote, `kan` - he was, `mshit` - I went, `sheftu` - I saw him).
  • How to use object pronoun suffixes (e.g., `shaf-ni` - he saw me, `3titu l-ktab` - I gave him the book).
  • Basic past time expressions (`l-bare7` - yesterday, `s-simana lli fatet` - last week).

Now, we'll focus on connecting these actions to tell a simple story.

Sequencing Words for Narratives

To tell a story, you need words that show the order of events.

English Darija (Script) Transcription Notes Audio
First / In the beginning ف اللول / أول حاجة f l-luwel / uwel 7aja `uwel 7aja` means "first thing"
Then / After that من بعد / ديك الساعة mn ba3d / dik s-sa3a `dik s-sa3a` means "that hour/time"
And then / And so و من بعد / و u mn ba3d / u `u` (and) can connect sequential actions.
Before قبل qbel e.g., `qbel ma nmshi` (before I go)
After بعد ba3d e.g., `ba3d ma klit` (after I ate)
Finally / In the end ف اللخر / ف التالي f l-lekher / f t-tali
`mn ba3d` (After that/Then): This is a very common and useful phrase for connecting steps in a story or sequence of events.

Example Simple Narrative

Let's look at a short story about what someone did yesterday. Pay attention to the past tense verbs and sequencing words.

البارح، ف اللول فقت بكري. من بعد، شربت قهوة و فطرت. و فطرت الخبز و الزيت. من بعد، مشيت للخدمة. فالخدمة، خدمت بزاف. ف اللخر، رجعت للدار و تعشيت.

L-bare7, f l-luwel feqt bekri. Mn ba3d, shrebt qhwa u ftert. U ftert l-khobz u z-zit. Mn ba3d, mshit l l-khedma. F l-khedma, khdemt bzzaf. F l-lekher, rje3t l d-dar u t3eshit.

Yesterday, first I woke up early. After that, I drank coffee and had breakfast. And I had bread and oil for breakfast. After that, I went to work. At work, I worked a lot. Finally, I returned home and had dinner.

Vocabulary from the Story:

  • فقت (feqt) - I woke up (from فاق - faq)
  • بكري (bekri) - early
  • فطرت (ftert) - I had breakfast (from فطر - fter)
  • الزيت (z-zit) - oil (olive oil)
  • الخدمة (l-khedma) - work/the job
  • خدمت (khdemt) - I worked (from خدم - khdem)
  • رجعت (rje3t) - I returned (from رجع - rje3)
  • تعشيت (t3eshit) - I had dinner (from تعشى - t3esha)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Order the Events

Imagine these actions happened. Put them in a logical order using sequencing words. Translate your short story.

Actions: (A) شربت قهوة (shrebt qhwa - I drank coffee), (B) مشيت للخدمة (mshit l l-khedma - I went to work), (C) فقت (feqt - I woke up).

Your Story (Darija): ف اللول فقت، من بعد شربت قهوة، و من بعد مشيت للخدمة.

Your Story (English): First I woke up, after that I drank coffee, and then I went to work.

Exercise 2: My Weekend

Write 2-3 sentences in Darija about what you did last weekend. Use past tense verbs and at least one sequencing word. (Example: L-weekend lli fat, zert 3a'ilti. Mn ba3d, tferrejt f wa7ed l-film.)

Your Sentences:

Exercise 3: Translate to Darija

  1. Yesterday, first I ate, then I went out.
    Answer: L-bare7, f l-luwel klit, mn ba3d khrejt. (klit from kla)
  2. She cooked tagine, and after that, we watched a film.
    Answer: Tiyybat ttajin, u mn ba3d tferrejna f wa7ed l-film. (tferrejna = we watched)
  3. Finally, I slept.
    Answer: F l-lekher, n3est.
