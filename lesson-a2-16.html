A2 Module 16: Review of Verb Tenses (Present, Past, Future)

Consolidate your understanding of the main verb tenses and practice using them in different contexts.

Verb Tense Overview

At the A2 level, you've learned how to talk about:

  • **Present Habitual Actions:** Using the `ka-` prefix (A1 Module 8).
  • **Past Completed Actions:** Using past tense suffixes (A2 Modules 1 & 2).
  • **Future Plans/Intentions:** Using `ghadi` + verb (A2 Module 5).

This module will help you distinguish between them and use them correctly.

Side-by-Side Comparison (Example: "to write" - `kteb`)

Let's compare the "Ana" (I) form for the verb `kteb` (كتب - to write) in all three tenses.

Tense Darija (Script) Transcription Meaning Audio
Present (`ka-`) كانكتب kankteb I write (habitually) / I am writing
Past (Simple) كتبت ktebt I wrote
Future (`ghadi`) غادي نكتب ghadi nkteb I will write / I am going to write

Key Indicators:

  • **Present `ka-`:** The `ka-` prefix is the main signal.
  • **Past Simple:** Characterized by suffixes like `-t`, `-ti`, `-u`, `-at`, `-na`, `-tu`. No prefix for regular verbs.
  • **Future `ghadi`:** The word `ghadi` (or `gha`) before the verb stem (which often has `n-`, `t-`, `y-` prefixes).

Context Clues: Time Expressions

Time expressions are crucial for understanding which tense is being used or which tense you should use.

Time Expression (Darija/Trans.) Typically Used With... Example Sentence (English)
كل نهار / kul nhar (every day) Present (`ka-`) I drink tea every day.
ديما / dima (always) Present (`ka-`) He always studies.
البارح / l-bare7 (yesterday) Past She went to the market yesterday.
السيمانة اللي فاتت / s-simana lli fatet (last week) Past We visited our family last week.
غدا / ghdda (tomorrow) Future (`ghadi`) They will travel tomorrow.
من بعد / mn ba3d (later / after) Future (`ghadi`) / Past (as sequencer) I will call you later. / After that, I slept.
دابا / daba (now) Present (`ka-` for ongoing/general) What are you doing now? (Shnu katdir daba?)
While `daba` (now) is present, Darija often uses the `ka-` form for actions happening "around now" or generally, rather than a distinct present continuous form like English "am/is/are -ing" for all situations.

Narrating a Simple Story (Mixed Tenses)

Let's try to use different tenses in a short narrative.

البارح، مشيت للسوق. شريت الخضرة و الفاكية. ملي رجعت للدار، طيبت واحد الطاجين بنين. كل نهار، كانفيق بكري و كانفطر مزيان. غدا، غادي نمشي عند صاحبي و غادي نتفرجو ف شي فيلم.

L-bare7, mshit l s-souq. Shrit l-khdra u l-fakiya. Melli rje3t l d-dar, tiyyebt wa7ed t-tajin bnin. Kul nhar, kanfiq bekri u kanfter mzyan. Ghdda, ghadi nmshi 3nd sa7bi u ghadi ntferrju f shi film.

Yesterday, I went to the market. I bought vegetables and fruit. When I returned home, I cooked a delicious tagine. Every day, I wake up early and I have a good breakfast. Tomorrow, I will go to my friend's place and we will watch a film.

Breakdown:

  • `mshit` (I went), `shrit` (I bought), `rje3t` (I returned), `tiyyebt` (I cooked) - **Past Tense**
  • `kanfiq` (I wake up), `kanfter` (I have breakfast) - **Present Tense (`ka-`)** for habitual actions (`kul nhar`)
  • `ghadi nmshi` (I will go), `ghadi ntferrju` (we will watch) - **Future Tense (`ghadi`)** for `ghdda` (tomorrow)

Exercise 1: Identify the Tense

For each Darija sentence, identify if it's Present (`ka-`), Past, or Future (`ghadi`).

  1. كايقرا كتاب كل ليلة. (Kayqra ktab kul lila.) - Tense: Present (ka-)
  2. سافرت لفرنسا العام اللي فات. (Safert l Fransa l-3am lli fat.) - Tense: Past
  3. غادي نتعشاو ف الريسطورة اليوم. (Ghadi nt3eshaw f r-restora l-yum.) - Tense: Future (ghadi)
  4. ما شفتوش البارح. (Ma sheftush l-bare7.) - Tense: Past (negated)
  5. ديما كانمشي للخدمة بكري. (Dima kanmshi l l-khedma bekri.) - Tense: Present (ka-)
