Module 11: Family Members

Learn the Darija words for common family members and how to talk about your family.

Talking About *Your* Family (Possessives)

Remember possessive suffixes from Module 3? They are very commonly attached directly to family member terms.

Using Direct Suffixes (Common)

English Darija Script Transcription Breakdown Audio
My father بابا / أبي baba / ab-i `baba` (no suffix needed) / `ab` + `-i`
My mother ماما / أمي mama / omm-i `mama` (no suffix needed) / `omm` + `-i`
My brother خويا khu-ya `khu` + `-ya` (slight change for "my")
My sister ختي kh-ti `kht` + `-i` (shortened stem)
Your (m.s.) brother خوك khu-k `khu` + `-k`
His wife مراتو mra-tu `mra` + `-t-` + `-u` (feminine noun rule)
Her husband راجلها rajl-ha `rajl` + `-ha`
Our children ولادنا wlad-na `wlad` + `-na`
`Baba` / `Mama`: These common terms already imply "my dad" / "my mom" for many speakers, so the `-i` suffix isn't usually added to them. For other people's parents, you'd use suffixes or `dyal`: `babak` (your dad), `mamak` (your mom), `baba-h` (his dad), `mama-ha` (her mom), or `l-ab dyalu` (his father), `l-omm dyalha` (her mother).
`Khu-ya` / `Kh-ti`: Note the common forms for "my brother" and "my sister" use slightly modified stems.

Using `dyal` (Also Possible)

While direct suffixes are very common with family, you can also use `dyal`. Structure: **[Family Member] + `dyal` + [Possessor]**

  • My father: الأب ديالي (l-ab dyali) - More formal
  • Your (m.s.) brother: الخو ديالك (l-khu dyalek)
  • Her husband: الراجل ديالها (r-rajl dyalha)
  • Fatima's sister: الخت ديال فاطمة (l-kht dyal Fatima)

Here are a few terms for extended family members. Don't worry about memorizing all of these immediately for A1.

Complexity: Arabic-based languages often have specific terms depending on whether the relative is from the mother's or father's side (e.g., `3emm` vs. `khal` for uncle). Cousins are described by relationship (e.g., "son of the uncle"). For A1, focus on the immediate family first.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Family Vocabulary

Write the Darija transcription for:

  1. Mother: mama / omm
  2. Brother: khu
  3. Son: weld
  4. Wife: mra

Exercise 2: Possessives

Translate these into Darija using direct suffixes where common, otherwise `dyal`.

  1. My sister: kh-ti
  2. His father: baba-h / l-ab dyalu
  3. Your (f.s.) mother: mama-k / l-omm dyalek
  4. Their children: wlad-hum / l-wlad dyalhum
  5. Ahmed's brother: khu Ahmed / l-khu dyal Ahmed

Exercise 3: Answering Questions

Answer these questions simply in Darija (imagine your own family):

  1. Wash 3ndek khut? (Answer: Iyyeh, 3ndi... / La, ma 3ndish.)
  2. Shnu smit mamak? (Answer: Smitha [Name].)
  3. Wash nta/nti mzewwej/mzewwja? (Answer: Iyyeh / La.)
Talking about family is a great way to practice possessives and basic vocabulary. Try describing your own family!
