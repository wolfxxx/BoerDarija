B1 Module 13: Expressing Emotions & Feelings

Learn vocabulary and common phrases to talk about your emotions and understand how others are feeling.

Basic Ways to Express Feelings

There are several common structures to talk about how you feel:

  • **Using Adjectives:** "Ana + [Adjective]" (I am [emotion]). E.g., `Ana fr7an.` (I am happy.)
  • **"Fiya..." (In me is...):** Used for certain sensations or strong feelings. E.g., `Fiya jou3.` (I am hungry. Lit: In me is hunger.)
  • **"Kan7ess b..." (I feel...):** `kan7ess b` (كانحس ب) + [Noun/Adjective]. E.g., `Kan7ess b l-fr7.` (I feel happiness.)

Using "Fiya..." (In me is...) for Sensations/Strong Feelings

This structure is very common for physical sensations and some strong emotions.

Structure: **`Fi-` + [Pronoun Suffix] + [Noun (the feeling/sensation)]**

  • `Fiya jou3.` (فيا الجوع.) - I am hungry.
  • `Fik l-3tesh?` (فيك العطش؟) - Are you (s.) thirsty?
  • `Fih s-skhana.` (فيه السخانة.) - He has a fever.
  • `Fiha l-khl3a.` (فيها الخلعة.) - She is scared/startled. (`l-khl3a` = fright/shock)
  • `Fina n-n3as.` (فينا النعاس.) - We are sleepy. (`n-n3as` = sleepiness)
Notice the pronoun suffixes: `fi-ya` (in me), `fi-k` (in you s.), `fi-h` (in him), `fi-ha` (in her), `fi-na` (in us), `fi-kum` (in you pl.), `fi-hum` (in them).

Using "Kan7ess b..." (I feel...)

The verb `7ess b-` (حس ب - to feel) is conjugated normally (e.g., `kan7ess`, `kat7ess`, `kay7ess`) and is followed by the preposition `b-` and then the feeling (often a noun).

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I feel happiness. كانحس بالفرح. Kan7ess b l-fre7.
She feels tired. كاتحس بالعيا. Kat7ess b l-3ya.
He feels a little pain. (`l-7riq` - pain) كايحس بشوية د الحريق. Kay7ess b shwiya d l-7riq.
How do you (m.s.) feel? باش كاتحس؟ / كيفاش كاتحس؟ Bash kat7ess? / Kifash kat7ess?

Dialogue: Checking In

Youssef: السلام فاطمة، لاباس؟ كايبان ليا ماشي هي هاديك.
Ssalām Fatima, labas? Kayban li-ya mashi hiya hadik.
Hello Fatima, how are you? You don't seem yourself. (Lit: It seems to me not she is that one)

Fatima: و عليكم السلام يوسف. شوية، أنا عيانة بزاف اليوم.
Wa 3likum ssalam Youssef. Shwiya, ana 3iyyana bzzaf l-yum.
Hello Youssef. A little (so-so), I am very tired today.

Youssef: أواه، مالك؟ واش فيك شي حاجة؟
Awah, malek? Wash fik shi 7aja?
Oh dear, what's wrong with you? Is something wrong? (Lit: Is in you some thing?)

Fatima: ما عرفتش، كانحس براسي ماشي مزيان. يمكن ضاربني البرد.
Ma 3reftsh, kan7ess b rasi mashi mzyan. Yemken Darbni l-berd.
I don't know, I feel not well. (Lit: I feel by my head not good). Maybe I caught a cold.

Youssef: الله يشافيك. خاصك ترتاحي.
Allah yshafik. Khassk trta7i.
May God heal you. You need to rest.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: How Do They Feel?

Complete the sentences using the adjective form or "fiya..." structure.

  1. Huwa (happy): Huwa fr7an.
  2. Ana (hungry): Fiya jou3. / Ana ji3an(a).
  3. Hiya (sad/upset): Hiya mqellqa / 7azina.
  4. Huma (scared): Huma khayfin. (Plural of khayf) / Fihum l-khl3a.
  5. Nta (thirsty): Fik l-3tesh. / Nta 3tshan.

Exercise 2: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences:

  1. I feel very tired today. Kan7ess b l-3ya bzzaf l-yum. / Ana 3iyyan(a) bzzaf l-yum.
  2. She said that she is angry. Qalet belli hiya m3essba.
  3. Are you (m.s.) surprised? Wash nta mndehesh/mtfaje'?
  4. We don't feel fear. Ma kan7essush b l-khuf. / Ma fina-sh l-khl3a.
Being able to express and understand emotions is key to deeper connections. Practice talking about how you feel using these different structures.
