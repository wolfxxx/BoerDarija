Module 1: Greetings, Politeness & Introductions

Learn the essential phrases for meeting people, being polite, and introducing yourself in Moroccan Darija.

Basic Greetings

Greetings are the first step to any conversation. Here are the most common ones:

English Darija Script Transcription Notes Audio
Hello / Peace be upon you السلام عليكم Ssalām 3likum Formal and very common.
(Reply to Ssalām 3likum) وعليكم السلام Wa 3likum ssalam And upon you peace.
Hi / Hello (less formal) سلام Slam Casual. Can be used among friends or in informal settings.
Good morning صباح الخير Sba7 l-khir
(Reply to Good morning) صباح النور Sba7 n-nur Morning of light.
Good evening/afternoon مساء الخير Msa l-khir
(Reply to Good evening) مساء النور Msa n-nur Evening of light.
Good night (when leaving) تصبح على خير Tsb7 3la khir (to a male)
Good night (when leaving) تصبحي على خير Tsb7i 3la khir (to a female)

Asking "How Are You?"

English Darija Script Transcription Notes Audio
How are you? (General) لاباس؟ Labas? Literally "No harm?". Very common.
How are you? (to a male) كي داير؟ Ki dayr?
How are you? (to a female) كي دايرة؟ Ki dayra?
Everything okay? كلشي بخير؟ Kulshi bikhir?

Common Replies:

Fine, thanks be to God. لاباس الحمد لله Labas, l-7amdullah.
Good / Fine. مزيان Mzyan.
Everything is good. كلشي مزيان Kulshi mzyan.
And you? (to a male) و نتا؟ U nta?
And you? (to a female) و نتي؟ U nti?
Cultural Tip: It's common to ask "Labas?" multiple times in a greeting sequence. Don't be surprised! It shows care and politeness.

Introducing Yourself & Others

Subject Pronouns (Singular)

You'll learn more about pronouns later, but here are the basics for introductions:

  • أنا (Ana) - I
  • نتا (Nta) - You (masculine singular)
  • نتي (Nti) - You (feminine singular)
  • هو (Huwa) - He
  • هي (Hiya) - She

Asking and Stating Names

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
What's your name? (to male) شنو سميتك؟ Shnu smitek?
What's your name? (to female) شنو سميتك؟ Shnu smitek?
My name is... سميتي... Smiti...
His name is... سميتو... Smitu...
Her name is... سميتها... Smitha...
Pleased to meet you. متشرفين Mtsherrfin
Pronunciation of "Smitek": While it's the same for male and female, the context of who you're speaking to makes it clear. "Smitek" comes from "Smiya dyalek" (name of yours).

Example Dialogue: Meeting Someone

Youssef: السلام عليكم
Ssalām 3likum
Hello

Fatima: وعليكم السلام
Wa 3likum ssalam
Hello

Youssef: لاباس؟
Labas?
How are you?

Fatima: لاباس الحمد لله. و نتا؟
Labas, l-7amdullah. U nta?
Fine, thanks to God. And you?

Youssef: مزيان، شكراً. أنا سميتي يوسف. شنو سميتك؟
Mzyan, shukran. Ana smiti Youssef. Shnu smitek?
Good, thank you. My name is Youssef. What's your name?

Fatima: سميتي فاطمة. متشرفين.
Smiti Fatima. Mtsherrfin.
My name is Fatima. Pleased to meet you.

Youssef: متشرفين.
Mtsherrfin.
Pleased to meet you too.

Saying Goodbye

English Darija Script Transcription Notes Audio
Goodbye بسلامة Bslama Most common way to say goodbye.
See you later نتشاوفو من بعد Ntshawfu mn ba3d Literally "We'll see each other later."
See you tomorrow نتشاوفو غدا Ntshawfu ghdda

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Fill in the Blanks

Imagine you meet someone new. How would you complete this short conversation?

  • You: السلام عليكم (Ssalām 3likum)
  • Other Person: وعليكم السلام (Wa 3likum ssalam)
  • You: لاباس؟ (Labas?)
  • Other Person: لاباس الحمد لله. و ________؟ (Labas l-7amdullah. U ____?) (Assuming you are male)
  • You: مزيان. أنا سميتي [Your Name]. شنو ________؟ (Mzyan. Ana smiti [Your Name]. Shnu ____?)
  • Other Person: سميتي [Their Name]. ________. (Smiti [Their Name]. ____.)

Exercise 2: Mini-Dialogues

How would you say the following in Darija?

  1. Good morning! How are you? (to a female)
  2. Thank you very much. You're welcome.
  3. What's his name? His name is Ali.
  4. Goodbye! See you tomorrow.
Next Steps: Practice these phrases out loud! The more you say them, the more natural they will become. In the upcoming modules, we'll start building more complex sentences.
