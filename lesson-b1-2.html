B1 Module 2: The Imperfect (Habitual Past / "Used To")

Learn how to talk about actions that used to happen regularly or were ongoing in the past using the "kan + ka-verb" structure.

Introducing the Imperfect Tense

The "imperfect" tense in Darija (though not always called this formally, it functions similarly to imperfect tenses in other languages) is used to describe:

  • Habitual actions in the past: Things you "used to do" regularly.
  • Ongoing actions in the past: Things you "were doing" when something else happened.
  • Descriptions of states or conditions in the past: How things "were."

It's formed by combining the past tense of the verb `kan` (كان - to be) with the present habitual `ka-` form of the main verb.

Structure: **[Conjugated `kan` in past tense] + [Main verb in `ka-` present form]**

Formation with `kan` + `ka-Verb`

First, let's quickly review the past tense of `kan` (from B1 Module 1):

  • `kunt` (I was)
  • `kunti` (You (s.) were)
  • `kan` (He was)
  • `kanet` (She was)
  • `kunna` (We were)
  • `kuntu` (You (pl.) were)
  • `kanu` (They were)

Now, we add the `ka-` form of the main verb. For example, with `kteb` (to write), its `ka-` forms are `kankteb` (I write), `katkteb` (you/she writes), `kaykteb` (he writes), etc.

Example: "to write" - `kteb` (كتب) in the Imperfect

Pronoun Imperfect Form (Darija Script) Transcription Meaning (I used to write / I was writing) Audio
Ana (I) كنت كانكتب kunt kankteb I used to write / I was writing
Nta (You m.s.) كنتي كاتكتب kunti katkteb You used to write / You were writing
Nti (You f.s.) كنتي كاتكتبي kunti katketbi You used to write / You were writing
Huwa (He) كان كايكتب kan kaykteb He used to write / He was writing
Hiya (She) كانت كاتكتب kanet katkteb She used to write / She was writing
7na (We) كنا كانكتبو kunna kanketbu We used to write / We were writing
Ntuma (You pl.) كنتو كاتكتبو kuntu katketbu You (pl) used to write / You were writing
Huma (They) كانو كايكتبو kanu kayketbu They used to write / They were writing
Consistency: The `kan` part conjugates for the person and past tense. The `ka-verb` part conjugates for the person in the present habitual form.

Examples in Context

Habitual Past ("Used to...")

  • ملي كنت صغير، كنت كانلعب الكورة كل نهار.
    Melli kunt sghir, kunt kanl3eb l-kura kul nhar.
    When I was young, I used to play football every day.
  • شحال هادي، كنا كانسافرو بزاف.
    Sh7al hadi, kunna kansafru bzzaf.
    A long time ago, we used to travel a lot. (Lit: "How much this," meaning "some time ago")
  • كان كايقرا ف هاديك المدرسة.
    Kan kayqra f hadik l-mdrasa.
    He used to study at that school.

Ongoing Past Action ("Was/Were -ing")

This usage often describes a background action that was happening when another, shorter action (simple past) interrupted it.

  • كنت كانتفرج ف التلفزة ملي دق الباب.
    Kunt kantferrej f t-telfaza melli deqq l-bab.
    I was watching TV when the door knocked. (`deqq l-bab` - the door knocked)
  • كانو كايهدرو ملي دخلت.
    Kanu kayhdru melli dkhalt.
    They were talking when I entered. (`dkhalt` - I entered)
Simple Past vs. Imperfect:
  • Simple Past (`ktebt` - I wrote): A completed action at a specific point in the past.
  • Imperfect (`kunt kankteb` - I used to write / was writing): A habitual action in the past, or an ongoing action in the past.

Negating the Imperfect

To negate the imperfect, you negate the `kan` part: `ma kunt-sh...`, `ma kan-sh...`, etc.

Structure: **`ma` + [Conjugated `kan` in past] + `sh` + [Main verb in `ka-` present form]**

  • I didn't used to play / I wasn't playing: ما كنتش كانلعب. (`ma kuntsh kanl3eb`)
  • He didn't used to study / He wasn't studying: ما كانش كايقرا. (`ma kansh kayqra`)
  • They didn't used to travel / They weren't traveling: ما كانوش كايسافرو. (`ma kanush kaysafru`)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Conjugate in Imperfect

Conjugate the verb `safr` (سافر - to travel) in the imperfect tense for:

  1. Ana (I): Kunt kansafr.
  2. Hiya (She): Kanet katsafr.
  3. Huma (They): Kanu kaysafru.
  4. Nta (You m.s. - Negative): Ma kuntish katsafr.

Exercise 2: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences using the imperfect tense:

  1. We used to live in Rabat. (`sken` - to live)
    Answer: Kunna kanskunu f Rbat.
  2. She was cooking when the phone rang. (`tiyyeb` - to cook; `souna t-tilifun` - the phone rang)
    Answer: Kanet kattiyyeb melli souna t-tilifun.
  3. I didn't used to like coffee. (`kay3jbni` for "I like")
    Answer: Ma kantsh kat3jbni l-qhwa. / Ma kuntsh kanbghi l-qhwa. (Using `bgha` is also common for "like" in past context like this)
  4. They were always playing outside. (`l3eb` - to play; `berra` - outside)
    Answer: Kanu dima kayl3bu berra.
The imperfect tense adds a lot of richness to your ability to talk about the past. Think about your childhood habits or what you were doing at a specific time yesterday and try to express it in Darija!
