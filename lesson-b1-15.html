B1 Module 15: Handling Problems & Expressing Complaints (Simple)

Learn how to politely express dissatisfaction, describe simple problems, and ask for solutions in everyday situations.

Identifying a Problem

First, you need to be able to state that there is an issue.

English Phrase Darija (Script) Transcription Explanation Audio
There is a problem. كاين مشكل. Kayen mushkil. `mushkil` (m) - problem
I have a problem. عندي مشكل. 3ndi mushkil.
This isn't working. / It's broken. (m./f.) هادا ما خدامش. / هادي ما خداماش. Hada ma khddam-sh. / Hadi ma khddama-sh. `khddam` (working - adj.)
It's broken/damaged. (m./f.) مهرس / مهرسة. mherres / mherrsa. Passive participle of `herres` (to break)
This isn't what I ordered/wanted. هادشي ماشي هو اللي بغيت. Hadshi mashi huwa lli bghit. `hadshi` - this thing

Expressing Dissatisfaction Politely

It's important to be polite even when complaining.

English Phrase Darija (Script) Transcription Explanation Audio
Excuse me, but... سمح ليا، ولكن... Sme7 li-ya, walakin... Polite way to start a complaint.
I am not happy with... / This doesn't please me... هادشي ما عجبنيش. Hadshi ma 3jebni-sh. Lit. "This thing didn't please me."
It's not good. / It's not okay. ماشي مزيان. Mashi mzyan.
This is not normal. هادشي ماشي نورمال. Hadshi mashi normal. `normal` (from French)

Asking for a Solution

English Phrase Darija (Script) Transcription Explanation Audio
What can we do? / What's the solution? شنو نديرو؟ / شنو الحل؟ Shnu ndiru? / Shnu l-7ell? `l-7ell` - the solution
Can you help me, please? واش يمكن ليك تعاوني، عافاك؟ Wash yemken lik t3awenni, 3afak? (Review from B1.4)
I want to change this (m./f.). بغيت نبدّل هادا / هادي. Bghit nbeddel hada / hadi. `nbeddel` - I change (from `beddel` - to change)
I want my money back, please. بغيت فلوسي، عافاك. / بغيت نرجع فلوسي. Bghit flusi, 3afak. / Bghit nrejje3 flusi. `nrejje3` - I return (Form II)
Can you repair it? (m./f.) واش يمكن ليك تصلحو / تصلحها؟ Wash yemken lik tSele7-u / tSele7-ha? `tSele7` - you repair (from `Sle7` Form I, but `SeLLe7` Form II is common for "to repair")
Being Understood: In situations where there's a problem, speaking clearly and calmly is important. Using polite phrases like `sme7 liya` and `3afak` can help.

Dialogue: Problem with a Purchased Item

Customer: السلام عليكم. سمح ليا، عندي واحد المشكل.
Ssalām 3likum. Sme7 li-ya, 3ndi wa7ed l-mushkil.
Hello. Excuse me, I have a problem.

Shopkeeper: و عليكم السلام. مالك؟ شنو كاين؟
Wa 3likum ssalam. Malek? Shnu kayen?
Hello. What's wrong with you? What is there?

Customer: شريت هاد التيليفون البارح من هنا، ولكن ما خدامش مزيان.
Shrit had t-tilifun l-bare7 mn hna, walakin ma khddam-sh mzyan.
I bought this phone yesterday from here, but it's not working well.

Shopkeeper: آه، وريني نشوف. بصح، كاين مشكل.
Ah, werrini nshuf. Bssa7, kayen mushkil.
Ah, show me let me see. True, there is a problem.

Customer: واش يمكن ليك تبدلو ليا، عافاك؟
Wash yemken lik tbeddel-u li-ya, 3afak?
Can you exchange it for me, please?

Shopkeeper: واخا، ماشي مشكل. خود واحد آخر.
Wakha, mashi mushkil. Khud wa7ed akher.
Okay, no problem. Take another one.

Customer: شكراً بزاف!
Shukran bzzaf!
Thank you very much!

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Stating the Problem

How would you say these in Darija?

  1. This food (f.) is not good. Had l-makla mashi mzyana.
  2. My watch (ساعة - sa3a, f.) is broken. Sa3ti mherrsa.
  3. There is a problem with the internet. Kayen mushkil f l-internet.

Exercise 2: Asking for a Solution

Translate these requests:

  1. I want to change these shoes (m. pl. `sbbat`). Bghit nbeddel had s-sbbat.
  2. Can you repair this radio (m.), please? Wash yemken lik tSele7 had r-radio, 3afak?
  3. I want my money back because this (m.) is not what I ordered.
    Bghit flusi 7it hadshi mashi huwa lli bghit / lli commandit.

Exercise 3: Mini-Scenario

You are at a restaurant. The soup (الحريرة - l-7rira, f.) is cold (باردة - barda). How do you politely tell the waiter and ask for a solution?

Your Darija: Sme7 liya, had l-7rira barda shwiya. Wash yemken lik tskhenha liya, 3afak? (tskhenha = you heat it (f.))

Handling problems politely is an important skill. Remember to use `sme7 liya` (excuse me) and `3afak` (please) to maintain a good tone.
