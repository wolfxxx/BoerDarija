A2 Module 6: More Prepositions (Time, Movement, Purpose)

Expand your ability to describe relationships between actions, time, and intentions with more prepositions.

Prepositions of Time

These prepositions help specify when an action occurs or the duration of something.

Preposition (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Sentence (Darija) Example Sentence (Transcription) English Translation Audio
قبل / qbel before كليت قبل ما نخرج. Klit qbel ma nkhruj. I ate before I went out.
بعد / ba3d after غادي نتفرج ف التلفزة بعد العشا. Ghadi ntferrej f t-telfaza ba3d l-3sha. I will watch TV after dinner.
ف وقت / f weqt at the time of, during ف وقت الغدا، كانهضر مع ماما. F weqt l-ghda, kanhder m3a mama. During lunch, I talk with my mom.
من... حتى ل... / mn... 7tta l... from... until/to... كانخدم من التمنية حتى للخمسة. Kankhdem mn t-tmnya 7tta l l-khmsa. I work from eight until five.
حتى / 7tta until, even غادي نتسناك حتى تجي. Ghadi ntsennak 7tta tji. I will wait for you until you come.

Prepositions of Movement & Direction (Review & Expand)

These build on what you learned about locations, focusing more on the action of moving.

Preposition (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Sentence (Darija) Example Sentence (Transcription) English Translation Audio
ل / l to (destination) مشيت ل كازا. Mshit l Kaza. I went to Casablanca.
من / mn from (origin) جيت من الخدمة. Jit mn l-khedma. I came from work.
فوق / foq over, above (can imply movement over) الطياؤة طارت فوق الجبال. T-tiyyara taret foq j-jbal. The plane flew over the mountains.
تحت / t7t under (can imply movement under) القط داز تحت الطموبيل. L-qett daz t7t t-ttomobil. The cat passed under the car.
على / 3la towards, onto (can imply direction) رميت الكرة على الحيط. Rmit l-kura 3la l-7it. I threw the ball towards/against the wall.

Prepositions of Purpose

These prepositions explain *why* an action is done.

Preposition (Darija/Trans.) Meaning Example Sentence (Darija) Example Sentence (Transcription) English Translation Audio
باش / bash in order to, so that, to كانقرا باش نتعلم. Kanqra bash nt3llem. I read in order to learn.
على ود / 3la wedd for the sake of, because of, for جيت على ودك. Jit 3la wedd-ek. I came for your sake / because of you.
ل / l for (benefit, purpose - less common than bash for verbs) شريت كادو لماما. Shrit kado l-mama. I bought a gift for mom.
`bash` + Verb: `bash` is very commonly followed by a verb (often in its present tense stem form, like `nt3llem` from `t3llem`).

Combining Prepositions with Pronoun Suffixes

As mentioned in A1, many prepositions can take pronoun suffixes to mean "to me," "with him," "for her," etc. This is a vital skill for A2.

Example with `l-` (to/for):

  • `liya` (ليا) - to/for me
  • `lik` (ليك) - to/for you (s.)
  • `lih` (ليه) - to/for him
  • `liha` (ليها) - to/for her
  • `lina` (لينا) - to/for us
  • `likum` (ليكم) - to/for you (pl.)
  • `lihum` (ليهم) - to/for them

Sentence: عطيتو ليك. (3titu lik.) - I gave it to you.

Example with `m3a-` (with):

  • `m3aya` (معايا) - with me
  • `m3ak` (معاك) - with you (s.)
  • `m3ah` (معاه) - with him

Sentence: واش غادي تمشي معاه؟ (Wash ghadi tmshi m3ah?) - Will you go with him?

Practice attaching suffixes to other prepositions like `f` (e.g., `fiya` - in me), `3la` (e.g., `3liya` - on me/about me).

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Choose the Preposition

Fill in the blank with an appropriate preposition (qbel, ba3d, mn...7tta l, bash, 3la wedd, f, 3la, l).

  1. Kanfiq qbel s-setta dyal s-sba7. (I wake up ____ six in the morning.)
  2. Ghadi nsafr ba3d s-simana j-jaya. (I will travel ____ next week.)
  3. Kayqra bash ydir امتحان (exam) مزيان. (He studies ____ do well on the exam.)
  4. Ana mshit l l-banka 3la wedd l-flus. (I went ____ the bank ____ money.)
  5. L-ktab dyali kayn 3la t-tbla f l-bit. (My book is ____ the table ____ the room.)

Exercise 2: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences, paying attention to prepositions:

  1. I will wait for you from 5 until 6. Ghadi ntsennak mn l-khmsa 7tta l s-setta.
  2. She came with her brother. Jat m3a khuha.
  3. I am doing this for you (m.s.). Kandir hadshi 3la weddek / lik.
  4. Before eating, I wash my hands. Qbel ma nakul, kanghsel yddiya. (kanghsel yddiya = I wash my hands)
Prepositions are powerful! They add a lot of detail and nuance to your sentences. Pay attention to how they are used in context.
