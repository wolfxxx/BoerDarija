A2 Module 12: Larger Numbers & More Complex Quantities

Expand your numerical skills by learning hundreds, thousands, and ways to express various quantities.

Review: Numbers 1-100

Quickly recall from A1 (Module 4) how to form numbers up to 100. Remember the pattern for 21-99 is [Unit] + `u` + [Ten].

  • 10: `3shra` (عشرة)
  • 19: `ts3tash` (تسعطاش)
  • 20: `3shrin` (عشرين)
  • 57: `sb3a u khmsin` (سبعة و خمسين)
  • 100: `mya` (مية)

Hundreds (`myat`)

To form hundreds, you use `mya` for 100. For 200, it's `myatayn`. For 300-900, you use the number (3-9) followed by `myat` (or `miyya` depending on region/speaker, `myat` is common).

Combining Hundreds with Other Numbers:

Pattern: **[Hundred] + `u` + [Number from 1-99]**

  • 101: `mya u wa7ed` (مية و واحد)
  • 250: `myatayn u khmsin` (ميتين و خمسين)
  • 325: `telt mya u khmsa u 3shrin` (تلت مية و خمسة و عشرين)
  • 789: `sb3 mya u ts3ud u tmanin` (سبع مية و تسعود و تمانين)

Thousands (`alaf`)

1000 is `alf` (ألف). 2000 is `alfeen` (ألفين). For 3000-10,000, use the number (3-10) + `alaf` (آلاف).

Number Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
1,000ألفalf
2,000ألفينalfayn
3,000تلات آلافtlata alaf
10,000عشرة آلاف3shra alaf

Combining Thousands with Other Numbers:

Pattern: **[Thousand] + `u` + [Number from 1-999]**

  • 1,500: `alf u khms mya` (ألف و خمس مية)
  • 2,023: `alfayn u tlata u 3shrin` (ألفين و تلاتة و عشرين)
  • 5,150: `khmsa alaf u mya u khmsin` (خمسة آلاف و مية و خمسين)
Reading Large Numbers: For very large numbers, Darija follows a similar pattern to English: say the thousands, then hundreds, then tens and units, connecting with `u` (and).

Expressions of Quantity

English Darija (Script) Transcription Notes Audio
A lot / Many / Very much بزاف bzzaf e.g., `flus bzzaf` (a lot of money)
A lot of... بزاف ديال... bzzaf dyal... e.g., `bzzaf dyal n-nas` (a lot of people)
A little / A bit / Some شوية shwiya e.g., `shwiya dyal l-ma` (a little water)
Some (of) شي shi e.g., `shi nas` (some people), `shi 7aja` (something)
All / Every كلشي / كل kulshi / kul `kulshi` (everything), `kul nhar` (every day)
Enough كافي / باراكا kafi / baraka `baraka` often means "enough, stop!"
Nothing والو / تا حاجة walu / ta 7aja Often used with negation: `ma kayn walu` (there is nothing)

Exercise 2: Quantities

  1. A lot of people. Bzzaf dyal n-nas.
  2. A little bit of sugar. Shwiya dyal s-skkar.
  3. I want some books. Bghit shi ktub.

Larger numbers and quantities are essential for shopping, understanding prices, and many other daily interactions. Keep practicing them!
