A2 Module 8: Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

Learn how to compare people, things, and actions, and how to identify the "most" or "least" of something.

Comparative Adjectives: More Than / Less Than

To say something is "more [adjective] than" something else, you use the adjective followed by `kter mn` (كتر من - more than).

Pattern: **[Noun 1] + [Adjective (agrees with Noun 1)] + `kter mn` + [Noun 2]**

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
This house is bigger than that house. هاد الدار كبيرة كتر من هاديك الدار. Had d-dar kbira kter mn hadik d-dar.
Ahmed is taller than Ali. أحمد طويل كتر من علي. Ahmed twil kter mn Ali.
This car is more expensive than my car. هاد الطموبيل غالية كتر من طموبيلتي. Had t-ttomobil ghalya kter mn ttomobilti.

To say something is "less [adjective] than" something else, you use `qell mn` (قل من - less than).

Pattern: **[Noun 1] + [Adjective (agrees with Noun 1)] + `qell mn` + [Noun 2]**

My book is smaller than your book. كتابي صغير قل من كتابك. Ktabi sghir qell mn ktabek.
Adjective Agreement: Remember, the adjective must always agree in gender and number with the first noun being described.

Comparative Adjectives: As... As

To say something is "as [adjective] as" something else, you use `b7al` (بحال - like, as) or `qed qed m3a` (قد قد مع - same size/level with).

Pattern 1: **[Noun 1] + [Adjective] + `b7al` + [Noun 2]** (Noun 2 often implied or a pronoun)

Pattern 2: **[Noun 1] + `qed qed m3a` + [Noun 2]** (Often for size/equality)

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
My house is as big as your house. داري كبيرة بحال دارك.
OR داري قد قد مع دارك.		 Dari kbira b7al darek.
OR Dari qed qed m3a darek.
He is as tall as his brother. هو طويل بحال خوه. Huwa twil b7al khuh.

Superlative Adjectives: The Most / The Least

To say something is "the most [adjective]" (e.g., the biggest, the tallest), you often use the structure:

Pattern: **`ahsan` (أحسن - best) / `akbar` (أكبر - biggest) / `asghar` (أصغر - smallest) + [Noun]** (These are common forms that function as superlatives)

OR: **` huwa/hiya/huma l-` + [Adjective with `a-` prefix pattern, e.g., `akbar`, `atwel`]**

OR for general "the most": **`كتر واحد(ة)` (kter wa7ed/wa7da - the most one) after the noun and adjective.**

English Darija (Script) Transcription Notes Audio
The biggest house. أكبر دار. / الدار الكبيرة كتر وحدة. Akbar dar. / D-dar l-kbira kter we7da. `Akbar` is an elative form.
The tallest man. أطول راجل. / الراجل الطويل كتر واحد. Atwal rajl. / R-rajl t-twil kter wa7ed. `Atwal` is an elative form.
The best book. أحسن كتاب. A7san ktab. `A7san` means best.
The most expensive car. الطموبيل الغالية كتر وحدة. / أغلى طموبيل. T-ttomobil l-ghalya kter we7da. / Aghla ttomobil. `Aghla` is an elative form.

To say "the least [adjective]", you can use `aqall` (أقل - least) or by negating the "most":

  • The least expensive car: أقل طموبيل غالية. (Aqall ttomobil ghalya.) OR الطموبيل اللي ماشي غالية بزاف. (T-ttomobil lli mashi ghalya bzzaf. - The car that is not very expensive.)
Elative Form: Many superlatives in Arabic (and Darija by extension) use a special adjective pattern called the "elative" form, often starting with `a-` (like `akbar` from `kbir`, `atwal` from `twil`). `A7san` (best) is a common one. For A2, recognizing `akbar`, `asghar`, and `a7san` is a good start. The "kter wa7ed/wa7da" construction is also very useful.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Make Comparisons (More Than)

Translate these sentences into Darija:

  1. My car (f.) is newer than your car (m.). (new: jdid/jdida)
    Answer: Tomobilti jdida kter mn tomobiltek.
  2. Coffee is more expensive than tea. (expensive: ghali/ghalya)
    Answer: L-qhwa ghalya kter mn atay.
  3. He is kinder than his sister. (kind: drif/drifa)
    Answer: Huwa drif kter mn khtu.

Exercise 2: As... As

Translate using `b7al` or `qed qed m3a`:

  1. This book is as interesting as that book. (interesting: ممتع mumti3 / ممتعة mumti3a)
    Answer: Had l-ktab mumti3 b7al hadak l-ktab.
  2. Fatima is as tall as her mother.
    Answer: Fatima twila b7al mamaha. / Fatima qed qed m3a mamaha f t-tul. (f t-tul = in height)

Exercise 3: Superlatives

How would you say the following in Darija?

  1. The smallest house. Asghar dar. / D-dar s-sghira kter we7da.
  2. The best student (m.). A7san talib.
  3. The most beautiful city (f.). A7san mdina. / L-mdina z-zwina kter we7da. (Using a7san for "most beautiful" is common, or zwina kter we7da).
Comparisons are a great way to add more detail to your conversations. Try comparing things around you or people you know!
