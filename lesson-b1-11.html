B1 Module 11: Reported Speech (Simple Statements & Questions)

Learn the basics of how to report what someone else said or asked in Moroccan Darija.

What is Reported Speech?

Reported speech (also called indirect speech) is when you tell someone what another person said, without quoting them directly. For example:

  • Direct Speech: Ali said, "I am tired."
  • Reported Speech: Ali said that he was tired.

In Darija, this often involves a reporting verb (like "to say" or "to ask") followed by the conjunction `belli` (بلي - that) for statements, or `wash` (واش - if/whether) for reported yes/no questions.

Reporting Statements with `qal belli...`

The most common verb for "to say" is `qal` (قال). To report a statement, you use:

Structure: **[Reporter] + [Conjugated `qal`] + `belli` + [Reported Statement (often with pronoun/tense changes)]**

Direct Speech (Said by...) Reported Speech (Darija Script) Transcription English of Reported Speech Audio
Ahmed: "Ana mrid." (I am sick.) أحمد قال بلي هو مريض. Ahmed qal belli huwa mrid. Ahmed said that he is sick.
Fatima: "Ghadi nsafr ghdda." (I will travel tomorrow.) فاطمة قالت بلي هي غادي تسافر غدا. Fatima qalet belli hiya ghadi tsafr ghdda. Fatima said that she will travel tomorrow.
You (m.s.) said: "Shrebt atay." (I drank tea.) قلتي بلي شربتي أتاي. Qelti belli shrebti atay. You said that you drank tea.
Pronoun & Tense Changes:
  • **Pronouns:** Often change to reflect the perspective of the reporter (e.g., "I" in direct speech might become "he/she" in reported speech).
  • **Tenses:** In English, tenses often "shift back" in reported speech (e.g., "I *am* tired" becomes "He said he *was* tired"). In Darija, for simple reporting, the tense in the reported clause often *remains the same* as the original statement if the reporting verb (`qal`) is in the past and the situation is still true or relevant. However, if reporting something that is no longer true, or if more nuance is needed, tense shifts can occur (this becomes more complex at higher levels). For B1, keeping the original tense (while changing pronouns) is a good starting point.

Reporting Yes/No Questions with `suwwel wash...`

To report a yes/no question, you typically use the verb `suwwel` (سوّل - to ask) followed by `wash` (واش - if/whether).

Structure: **[Reporter] + [Conjugated `suwwel`] + [Person Asked (optional, e.g., `suwwel-ni` - he asked me)] + `wash` + [Reported Question (as a statement)]**

Direct Question (Asked by...) Reported Question (Darija Script) Transcription English of Reported Question Audio
Ali: "Wash nta mrid?" (Are you (m.s.) sick?) علي سوّلني واش أنا مريض. Ali suwwel-ni wash ana mrid. Ali asked me if I am sick.
Layla: "Wash ghadi tji?" (Will you (s.) come?) ليلى سوّلاتو واش غادي يجي. Layla suwwlat-u wash ghadi yji. Layla asked him if he will come.
The reported question part after `wash` becomes a statement (no question intonation or word order change needed there). Pronoun changes are still important.

Reporting Wh-Questions (Brief Introduction)

Reporting Wh-questions (who, what, where, when, why, how) is more complex as it involves keeping the question word and sometimes adjusting the verb structure. For B1, we'll just touch on the idea.

Structure: **[Reporter] + [Conjugated `suwwel`] + [Question Word] + [Rest of reported clause (often rephrased)]**

Example Direct: Ahmed: "Shnu derti l-bare7?" (What did you do yesterday?)

Example Reported: أحمد سوّلني شنو درت البارح. (Ahmed suwwel-ni shnu dert l-bare7.) - Ahmed asked me what I did yesterday.

The word order in the reported Wh-question often remains similar to the direct question, but without the question intonation. This will be explored more in higher levels.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Report the Statement

Change the direct speech to reported speech using `qal belli...`.

  1. Nadia said: "Ana fer7ana." (I am happy.)
    Reported: Nadia qalet belli hiya fer7ana.
  2. Omar and Ali said: "7na ghadi nsafru." (We will travel.)
    Reported: Omar u Ali qalu belli huma ghadi ysafru.
  3. You (f.s.) said: "3ndi ktab jdid." (I have a new book.)
    Reported: Qelti belli 3ndek ktab jdid.

Exercise 2: Report the Yes/No Question

Change the direct question to reported speech using `suwwel wash...`.

  1. He asked me: "Wash bghiti atay?" (Do you want tea?)
    Reported: Suwwelni wash bghit atay.
  2. She asked him: "Wash fhemti d-ders?" (Did you (m.s.) understand the lesson?)
    Reported: Suwwlatu wash fhem d-ders.

Exercise 3: From Reported to Direct (Challenge!)

What might have been the original direct speech?

  1. هو قال ليا بلي غادي يجي معطل. (Huwa qal liya belli ghadi yji m3ettel.) - He told me that he will come late.
    Direct Speech: "Ana ghadi nji m3ettel." (Said by him)
  2. هي سوّلاتني واش كانعجبها. (Hiya suwwlatni wash kan3jbha.) - She asked me if I like her. (This is ambiguous with `kay3jbni` structure, let's simplify or rephrase for clarity. A better example: She asked me if I know the answer. `Hiya suwwlatni wash kan3ref l-jawab.` )
    Let's use: هي سوّلاتني واش قريت الكتاب. (Hiya suwwlatni wash qrit l-ktab.) - She asked me if I read the book.
    Direct Speech: "Wash qriti l-ktab?" (Asked to you)
Reported speech is a key skill for recounting conversations and sharing information. Pay attention to pronoun changes!
