Module 14: Imperatives (Commands)

Learn how to give instructions and make requests by telling people to do (or not do) things in Darija.

Affirmative Commands (Telling Someone TO Do Something)

The imperative form is used to give commands. It changes depending on whether you're talking to one male (m.s.), one female (f.s.), or a group of people (plural).

Often, the masculine singular command is the simplest form, sometimes just the verb root.

Verb (Meaning) Command (to m.s.) Command (to f.s.) Command (to Plural) Audio (m.s/f.s/pl)
shrb (to drink) شرَب (shrb) شرَبي (shrbi) شرَبو (shrbu)
kul (to eat) كول (kul) كولي (kuli) كولو (kulu)
msha (to go) سير (sir) سيري (siri) سيرو (siru)
ja (to come) أجي (aji) أجي (aji) أجيو (ajiw)
shaf (to see/look) شوف (shuf) شوفي (shufi) شوفو (shufu)
kteb (to write) كتَب (kteb) كتَبي (ketbi) كتَبو (ketbu)
qra (to read) قرا (qra) قراي (qray) قراو (qraw)
Pattern Spotting:
  • Often, the command to a female (f.s.) adds an `-i` ending.
  • Often, the command to a plural group adds an `-u` ending.
  • Some verbs are irregular (like "go" and "come") and must be memorized.

Adding Politeness: `3afak`

To make a command polite (like adding "please"), simply add `3afak` (عافاك) either before or after the command.

  • Drink water, please (to m.s.): شرَب الما، عافاك. (Shrb l-ma, 3afak.)
  • Come here, please (to f.s.): عافاك أجي لهنا. (3afak aji l-hna.) (lhna = here)

Negative Commands (Telling Someone NOT To Do Something)

To tell someone not to do something, you use the `ma-...-sh` structure learned in Module 13, usually applied to the *present tense 'you' form* of the verb (e.g., `katshrb` - you drink becomes `ma tshrbs_h_`).

Pattern: **`ma` + [Present Tense "You" Stem] + `sh`**

Verb (Meaning) Negative Command (to m.s./f.s.) Negative Command (to Plural) Audio (m.s./pl)
shrb (to drink) ما تشرَبش (ma tshrbs_h_) ما تشرَبوش (ma tshrbu_sh_)
kul (to eat) ما تاكُلش (ma takul_sh_) ما تاكُلوش (ma takulu_sh_)
msha (to go) ما تمشيش (ma tmshi_sh_) ما تمشيوش (ma tmshiw_sh_)
ja (to come) ما تجيش (ma tji_sh_) ما تجيوش (ma tjiw_sh_)
kteb (to write) ما تكتَبش (ma tkteb_sh_) ما تكتَبوش (ma tktebu_sh_)
Singular Forms (m.s. vs f.s.): While the transcription often looks the same for `ma tshrbs_h_` (don't drink - m.s./f.s.), there can be subtle differences in the vowel sounds or emphasis depending on who you're speaking to. For A1, using the base `ma t-...-sh` form for singular is usually understood. The plural `ma t-...-ush` is distinct.

Examples:

  • Don't eat that! (to plural): ما تاكلوش هاداك! (Ma takulush hadak!)
  • Don't go to the market! (to f.s.): ما تمشيش للسوق! (Ma tmshish l s-souq!)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Give Affirmative Commands

Write the correct affirmative command form:

  1. (To m.s.) Look! Shuf!
  2. (To f.s.) Drink! Shrbi!
  3. (To Plural) Eat! Kulu!
  4. (To m.s.) Go! Sir!
  5. (To f.s.) Write! Ketbi!

Exercise 2: Give Negative Commands

Write the correct negative command form:

  1. (To m.s.) Don't go! Ma tmshish!
  2. (To Plural) Don't drink! Ma tshr bush!
  3. (To f.s.) Don't eat! Ma takulish! (or ma takulsh)
  4. (To m.s./f.s.) Don't look! Ma tshufsh!

Exercise 3: Translate the Command

Translate these commands into Darija:

  1. (To f.s.) Come here, please. Aji l-hna, 3afak.
  2. (To Plural) Don't write on the table! Ma tktebush 3la t-tbla!
  3. (To m.s.) Read the book! Qra l-ktab!
Giving commands is direct. Always remember to use `3afak` (please) to be polite, especially when asking someone to do something for you.
