A2 Module 1: A1 Review & Introduction to Past Tense

Welcome to A2! Let's refresh key A1 concepts and dive into talking about the past.

Quick A1 Review

Before we learn new things, let's briefly recall some important structures from A1:

  • **Greetings:** `Ssalām 3likum`, `Labas?`, `Shnu smitek?`
  • **`3ndi` (I have):** `3ndi ktab` (I have a book).
  • **`Bghit` (I want):** `Bghit l-ma` (I want water).
  • **Present Tense `ka-`:** `Kanakul l-khobz` (I eat bread).
  • **Negation:** `Ma kanakulsh` (I don't eat), `Hada mashi ktab` (This isn't a book), `Ma 3ndish flus` (I don't have money).
  • **Basic Questions:** `Wash...?`, `Shnu?`, `Fin?`, `Bsh7al?`
  • **Nouns & Adjectives:** Gender agreement, e.g., `dar kbira` (a big house).

If any of these feel rusty, take a moment to revisit the relevant A1 modules.

Introduction to the Simple Past Tense (`l-madi l-basit`)

The simple past tense is used to talk about actions or events that were completed in the past. In Darija, it's formed by changing the verb endings based on the person doing the action (the subject).

Unlike the `ka-` present tense, there's usually no prefix for the past tense with regular verbs.

Past Tense Suffixes (General Pattern)

Here's a general idea of the suffixes you'll be adding to the verb root:

  • **I:** `-t` (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb-t` - I drank)
  • **You (m.s.):** `-ti` (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb-ti` - You drank)
  • **You (f.s.):** `-ti` (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb-ti` - You drank)
  • **He:** (Often just the root, or root + `a`) (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb` - He drank)
  • **She:** `-at` (e.g., `shreb` → `shrb-at` - She drank)
  • **We:** `-na` (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb-na` - We drank)
  • **You (pl.):** `-tu` (e.g., `shreb` → `shreb-tu` - You (pl) drank)
  • **They:** `-u` (e.g., `shreb` → `shrb-u` - They drank)
Vowel Changes: The vowels within the verb root can sometimes change when adding suffixes. This is especially true for verbs with "weak" middle or final letters. We'll start with more regular examples.

Basic Past Time Expressions

To talk about when something happened in the past, use these expressions:

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
Yesterdayالبارحl-bare7
The day before yesterdayول البارحwel l-bare7
Last weekالسيمانة اللي فاتتs-simana lli fatet
Last monthالشهر اللي فاتsh-shher lli fat
Last yearالعام اللي فاتl-3am lli fat
Before / Agoقبل / هاديqbel / hadie.g., `qbel simana` (a week ago), `hadi telt yam` (three days ago)

Example Sentences:

  • I wrote a letter yesterday: كتبت برا البارح. (Ktebt bra l-bare7.) (bra = letter)
  • She drank tea last week: شربات أتاي السيمانة اللي فاتت. (Shrbat atay s-simana lli fatet.)
  • He ate couscous three days ago: كلا كسكسو هادي تلت يام. (Kla ksksu hadi telt yam.) (kla=he ate)
Word Order: Time expressions can often go at the beginning or end of the sentence in Darija, similar to English.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Conjugate in Past Tense

Conjugate the verb `fhm` (فهم - to understand) in the past tense for the following pronouns:

  1. Ana: fhemt
  2. Huwa: fhem
  3. Hiya: fhemat
  4. 7na: fhemna
  5. Huma: fhemu

Exercise 2: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences using the past tense:

  1. I drank coffee yesterday. Shrebt l-qhwa l-bare7.
  2. She wrote a book last year. Ketbat ktab l-3am lli fat.
  3. Did you (m.s.) eat tagine? (Wash...) Wash klti ttajin? (kliti from kla 'to eat')
  4. They understood the lesson (d-ders). Fhemu d-ders.
