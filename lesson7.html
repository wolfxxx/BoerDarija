Module 7: The Verb "To Want" (`bghit`), Food & Drink

Learn how to express what you want in Darija, and basic vocabulary for common food and drinks.

The Verb "To Want": `bgha` (بغى)

The verb "to want" in Darija is `bgha`. It's a bit irregular. For the present tense (expressing a current want), it often uses a form that looks like a past tense conjugation in other verbs. This is common for verbs of state or desire.

The most common form you'll use for "I want" is `bghit` (بغيت).

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
I want / I wanted بغيت bghit
You want / You wanted (m.s.) بغيتي bghiti
You want / You wanted (f.s.) بغيتي bghiti
He wants / He wanted بغى bgha
She wants / She wanted بغات bghat
We want / We wanted بغينا bghina
You want / You wanted (plural) بغيتو bghitu
They want / They wanted بغاو bghaw
Present vs. Past: While these forms can also mean "wanted" in the past, in A1 contexts, when you say `bghit ...` it's almost always understood as "I want..." (a current desire). Context clarifies if it's past.

Using `bghit` with Nouns

To say you want something (a noun), the structure is simple: **[Conjugated form of `bgha`] + [Noun]**

English Sentence Darija Script Transcription Audio
I want water. بغيت الما. Bghit l-ma.
She wants tea. بغات أتاي. Bghat atay.
Do you (m.s.) want coffee? واش بغيتي القهوة؟ Wash bghiti l-qhwa?

Definite Article `l-`: Often, when requesting food or drink, you'll use the definite article `l-` (the) if it's a common, expected item, e.g., `bghit l-ma` (I want the water / I want some water). If you want a specific type, you'd add an adjective: `bghit l-ma bard` (I want cold water).

Asking "What do you want?"

English Darija Script Transcription Audio
What do you want? (to m.s.) شنو بغيتي؟ Shnu bghiti?
What do you want? (to f.s.) شنو بغيتي؟ Shnu bghiti?
What do you (plural) want? شنو بغيتو؟ Shnu bghitu?

Expressing "I don't want"

To negate `bghit`, you use the `ma-...-sh` structure you'll learn more about in the Negation module. For now, here's the pattern for "I don't want":

ما بغيتش (ma bghitsh) - I don't want.

  • I don't want coffee: ما بغيتش القهوة. (Ma bghitsh l-qhwa.)

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: What Do They Want?

Complete the sentences in Darija (transcription):

  1. He wants bread. Bgha l-khobz.
  2. We want tea. Bghina atay.
  3. Do you (f.s.) want milk? Wash bghiti l-7lib?
  4. They don't want sugar. Ma bghawsh s-skkar. (using the plural form of bgha + negation)

Exercise 2: Your Order

Imagine you are at a café. How would you say the following in Darija?

  1. I want coffee with milk. (coffee with milk: قهوة بالحليب qhwa b-l-7lib) Bghit qhwa b-l-7lib.
  2. I want tea without sugar. (without sugar: بلا سكر bla skkar) Bghit atay bla skkar.
  3. What do you (m.s.) want? I want an orange. Shnu bghiti? Bghit limun.
Politeness: Remember to add عافاك (3afak - please) when making requests! For example: بغيت الما، عافاك. (Bghit l-ma, 3afak. - I want water, please.)
