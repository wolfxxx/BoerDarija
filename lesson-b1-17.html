B1 Module 17: Extended Narratives & Storytelling

Practice combining verb tenses, connectors, and descriptive language to tell more detailed stories about past experiences.

Building a Narrative

Storytelling involves more than just listing events. Good narratives often include:

  • Setting the Scene: When and where did it happen? Who was involved?
  • Sequence of Events: What happened first, next, then, finally? (Using connectors from B1.M10)
  • Details & Descriptions: Using adjectives, relative clauses (`lli`), and adverbs to make the story more vivid.
  • Feelings & Reactions: How did you or others feel? (Using language from B1.M13)
  • A Climax or Main Point: What was the most important or interesting part?
  • A Conclusion or Resolution: How did it end?

At the B1 level, your narratives will be simpler, but you can start incorporating these elements.

Key Elements for Storytelling (Review)

1. Past Tenses:

  • **Simple Past:** For completed actions (e.g., `mshit` - I went, `kla` - he ate, `shafet` - she saw). (A2.M1, A2.M2, B1.M1)
  • **Imperfect (Habitual/Ongoing Past):** For background actions or "used to" scenarios (e.g., `kunt kanl3eb` - I used to play / I was playing). (B1.M2)

2. Sequencing Words & Connectors:

  • `f l-luwel` (first), `mn ba3d` (after that/then), `u` (and), `walakin` (but), `7it` (because), `dakshi 3lash` (that's why), `f l-lekher` (finally). (A2.M12, B1.M10)
  • `melli` (when - conjunction, e.g., `melli kunt sghir` - when I was young).

3. Descriptive Language:

  • Adjectives + agreement (A1.M6, A2.M7).
  • Relative clauses with `lli` (A2.M13, B1.M7).

4. Expressing Feelings:

  • `Ana fr7an/mqelleq...` (I am happy/worried...).
  • `Fiya l-khl3a` (I am scared).
  • `Kan7ess b...` (I feel...). (B1.M13)

Example Extended Narrative: A Memorable Trip

Listen to or read the following story about a trip, paying attention to how different tenses and connectors are used.

العام اللي فات، سافرت أنا و عائلتي لمدينة إفران. ف اللول، كنا فرحانين بزاف حيت كانت أول مرة كانمشيو لتماك. مللي وصلنا، الجو كان بارد و كانت الشتا كاتصب شوية. ولكن، المدينة كانت زوينة بزاف، و الديور ديالها اللي بالسطوحة حمرين كانو كايحمقو.

من بعد، مشينا لواحد الغابة قريبة سميتها عين ڤيتل. تماك شفنا القرودة و عطيناهم ياكلو. كانت واحد التجربة واعرة. ف العشية، رجعنا ل لوطيل و تعشينا ماكلة بنينة.

ف الصراحة، دوزنا وقت زوين بزاف و كانتمنى نرجع ل إفران مرة خرى إن شاء الله.

L-3am lli fat, safert ana u 3a'ilti l mdinet Ifran. F l-luwel, kunna fr7anin bzzaf 7it kanet uwel mrra kanmshiw l temmak. Melli wselna, l-jeww kan bard u kanet sh-shta katSubb shwiya. Walakin, l-mdina kanet zwina bzzaf, u d-dyur dyalha lli b s-stu7a 7emrin kanu kay7emqu.

Mn ba3d, mshina l wa7ed l-ghaba qriba smitha Ain Vitel. Temmak shefna l-qruda u 3tinahum yaklu. Kanet wa7ed t-tejriba wa3ra. F l-3shiya, rje3na l l'otel u t3eshina makla bnina.

F s-sara7a, dewwezna weqt zwin bzzaf u kantmenna nerje3 l Ifran mrra khra Insha'Allah.

Last year, my family and I traveled to the city of Ifrane. At first, we were very happy because it was the first time we were going there. When we arrived, the weather was cold and it was raining a little. But, the city was very beautiful, and its houses which have red roofs were amazing.

After that, we went to a nearby forest called Ain Vitel. There we saw monkeys and we gave them (food) to eat. It was an awesome experience. In the evening, we returned to the hotel and had a delicious dinner.

Honestly, we had a very good time and I hope to return to Ifrane another time, God willing.

Key Narrative Elements Used:

  • **Time Setting:** `L-3am lli fat` (Last year)
  • **Sequencing:** `F l-luwel` (At first), `Melli wselna` (When we arrived), `Mn ba3d` (After that), `F l-3shiya` (In the evening), `F s-sara7a` (Honestly - for concluding thought).
  • **Past Tenses:** `safert` (I traveled), `kunna fr7anin` (we were happy - imperfect), `kanet uwel mrra` (it was the first time), `kanmshiw` (we go - imperfect for habitual context of "first time we *were going*"), `wselna` (we arrived), `kan bard` (it was cold), `kanet sh-shta katSubb` (it was raining - imperfect), `mshina` (we went), `shefna` (we saw), `3tinahum` (we gave them), `kanet tejriba` (it was an experience), `rje3na` (we returned), `t3eshina` (we had dinner), `dewwezna` (we spent/had time).
  • **Description:** `zwina bzzaf` (very beautiful), `dyur dyalha lli b s-stu7a 7emrin` (its houses which have red roofs), `ghaba qriba` (nearby forest), `tejriba wa3ra` (awesome experience), `makla bnina` (delicious food).
  • **Feelings/Hopes:** `kunna fr7anin` (we were happy), `kantmenna nerje3` (I hope to return).

Practice Time: Your Story

Prompt: A Memorable Day

Think about a memorable day you had recently or in the past (e.g., a birthday, a trip, an interesting encounter, a funny incident). Try to write a short story (4-6 sentences) in Darija describing it.

Try to include:

  • When it happened (e.g., `l-bare7`, `s-simana lli fatet`, `sh7al hadi`).
  • At least 2-3 different past tense verbs.
  • At least 2 sequencing words (e.g., `f l-luwel`, `mn ba3d`, `f l-lekher`).
  • At least one descriptive adjective or a simple relative clause with `lli`.
  • Optionally, how you felt.

Tips for Your Story:

  • Keep sentences relatively simple at first.
  • Focus on clear sequencing of events.
  • Use vocabulary you are comfortable with, then try to add one or two new words if you can.
  • Don't worry about perfection! The goal is practice and communication.
The more you practice telling simple stories, the more comfortable you'll become with linking ideas and using different tenses. You can even try telling the story out loud to yourself or a language partner.
