B1 Module 7: Advanced Relative Clauses & `ashmn` (Which)

Build more complex descriptive sentences using `lli` with prepositions, and learn to use `ashmn` to specify "which" item.

Review: Simple Relative Clauses with `lli`

In A2 (Module 13), you learned to use `lli` (اللي) to mean "that/which/who" when referring to a definite noun:

  • الراجل اللي جا. (R-rajl lli ja.) - The man who came.
  • الكتاب اللي قريت. (L-ktab lli qrit.) - The book that I read. (Sometimes `qritu` with resumptive pronoun)

Now we'll explore more complex uses.

Relative Clauses with Prepositions

When the noun being described by the `lli` clause is the object of a preposition within that clause, the preposition often comes *before* `lli`, and a resumptive pronoun suffix (referring back to the noun) is attached to the preposition.

Structure: **[Noun] + [Preposition + `lli` + Resumptive Pronoun Suffix] + [Rest of Clause]**

This can be a bit tricky, so let's look at examples. The resumptive pronoun matches the noun `lli` refers to.

English Darija (Script) Transcription Breakdown Audio
The house in which I live. الدار فاللي كانسكن فيها. D-dar felli kanskun fiha. The house + in which + I live in it. (`fiha` = in her/it (fem.))
The man with whom I traveled. الراجل ماللي سافرت معاه. R-rajl melli safert m3ah. The man + with whom + I traveled with him. (`m3ah` = with him)
The city to which we went. المدينة للي مشينا ليها. L-mdina lli mshina liha. The city + to which + we went to it. (`liha` = to her/it (fem.))
The pen with which I write. الستيلو باش كانكتب بيه. S-stilo bash kankteb bih. The pen + with which + I write with it. (`bih` = with it (masc.)) `bash lli -> bash` or `b'ash`
Contractions: Prepositions often contract with `lli`.
  • `f` + `lli` → `felli` (فاللي) or sometimes just `f lli`
  • `m3a` + `lli` → `melli` (مللي) (can also mean "when") or `m3a lli`
  • `l` + `lli` → `lli` (the `l` can be absorbed) or `l lli`
  • `b` + `lli` can become `belli` (باللي), but for "with which," `b'ash` (باش) is more common where `ash` means "what/which thing."
The resumptive pronoun suffix on the preposition at the end of the clause (e.g., `fiha`, `m3ah`, `liha`, `bih`) is very important for clarity.

Using `ashmn` (أشمن - Which)

`ashmn` is a determiner used before a noun to ask "which [noun]...?" or to specify "which [noun]..." in a statement when there are multiple options.

`ashmn` in Questions:

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
Which book do you want? أشمن كتاب بغيتي؟ Ashmn ktab bghiti?
Which car is yours (m.s.)? أشمن طموبيل ديالك؟ Ashmn ttomobil dyalek?

`ashmn` in Statements (often combined with `lli`):

To specify "the one which..." from a set of options.

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
I don't know which man came. ما عرفتش أشمن راجل اللي جا. Ma 3reftsh ashmn rajl lli ja.
Show me which house you bought. (Show me: `werrini`) وريني أشمن دار اللي شريتي. Werrini ashmn dar lli shriti.
`ashmn` helps narrow down choices. It's like saying "which one of these...?"

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Relative Clauses with Prepositions

Combine the sentences using a preposition + `lli` + resumptive pronoun.

  1. This is the pen. I write with the pen. (`b` - with)
    Darija: Hada s-stilo b'ash kankteb bih. / Hada s-stilo lli kankteb bih.
  2. Where is the café? We met at the café. (`f` - at/in)
    Darija: Fin l-qhwa felli tlaqina fiha? / Fin l-qhwa lli tlaqina fiha? (`tlaqina`=we met)
  3. The friend with whom I traveled is nice. (`m3a` - with)
    Darija: S-sa7b melli safert m3ah drif. / S-sa7b lli safert m3ah drif.

Exercise 2: Using `ashmn`

Translate these questions/statements to Darija using `ashmn`.

  1. Which color do you like? (Color: لون lun)
    Answer: Ashmn lun kay3jbek?
  2. I want to know which train goes to Rabat. (Train: تران tran)
    Answer: Bghit n3ref ashmn tran lli kaymshi l Rbat. (`n3ref`=I know)
  3. Which food (f.) did she cook? (Food: ماكلة makla)
    Answer: Ashmn makla lli tiyybat?
These advanced relative clauses can be challenging but are key to forming more natural and complex Darija sentences. Pay close attention to the resumptive pronouns!
