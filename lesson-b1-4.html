B1 Module 4: Expressing Ability, Possibility & Permission

Learn how to talk about what you can do, what might happen, and how to ask for and give permission.

Expressing Ability: `qder` (قدر - to be able to / can)

The verb `qder` is used to express ability or capability. It's followed by another verb, usually in its present stem form (often starting with `n-`, `t-`, `y-` depending on the subject).

Structure: **[Conjugated `qder`] + [Verb Stem (often with n-/t-/y- prefix)]**

`qder` in Present Tense (`ka-` form):

Pronoun Darija (Script) Transcription Meaning (I can / am able to...) Audio
AnaكانقدرkanqderI can / I am able to
Nta/NtiكاتقدرkatqderYou can / are able to
HuwaكايقدرkayqderHe can / is able to
HiyaكاتقدرkatqderShe can / is able to
7naكانقدروkanqdruWe can / are able to
NtumaكاتقدروkatqdruYou (pl) can / are able to
HumaكايقدروkayqdruThey can / are able to

Example: كانقدر نهضر بالدارجة شوية. (Kanqder nhder b d-darija shwiya.) - I can speak a little Darija.

Example: واش كاتقدر تعاوني؟ (Wash katqder t3awenni?) - Can you (s.) help me?

`qder` in Past Tense:

(Conjugates like a regular past tense verb: `qdert`, `qderti`, `qder`, `qdret`, `qderna`, `qdertu`, `qdru`)

Example: البارح قدرت نفيق بكري. (L-bare7 qdert nfiq bekri.) - Yesterday I was able to wake up early.

Example: ما قدرش يجي. (Ma qdersh yji.) - He couldn't come.

Expressing Possibility: `yemken` (يمكن - it's possible / maybe)

`yemken` is an impersonal expression meaning "it is possible" or "maybe." It's often followed by a verb (with pronoun prefix) or a full clause.

Structure 1: **`yemken` + [Verb (with n-/t-/y- prefix for subject)]**

Structure 2: **`yemken` + [Pronoun Suffix] + [Verb (with n-/t-/y- prefix for subject)]** (e.g., `yemken-li` - it's possible for me)

Structure 3: **`yemken` + `belli` + [Full Clause]**

English Darija (Script) Transcription Audio
Maybe I will go. / It's possible I will go. يمكن نمشي. Yemken nmshi.
It's possible for you (s.) to help. يمكن ليك تعاون. Yemken lik t3awen.
Maybe he is sick. يمكن بلي هو مريض. Yemken belli huwa mrid.
Maybe it will rain tomorrow. يمكن غادي طيح الشتا غدا. Yemken ghadi ti7 sh-shta ghdda.
`Yemken li / lik / lih...` etc.: This structure `yemken` + preposition `l-` + pronoun suffix emphasizes "it's possible *for someone* to do something." It's very common.
Example: `Yemken liya nsafr` - It's possible for me to travel / I might travel.

Asking for & Giving Permission

Here are common ways to ask if you can do something and how to respond.

English Phrase Darija (Script) Transcription Notes Audio
Can I...? / Is it possible for me to...? واش يمكن ليا...؟ / واش نقدر...؟ Wash yemken li-ya...? / Wash nqder...? Followed by verb stem (e.g., `ndkhol` - I enter)
Can I sit here? واش يمكن ليا نجلس هنا؟ Wash yemken li-ya ngles hna?
Yes, you can. / It's possible. إييه، يمكن ليك. / واخا. Iyyeh, yemken lik. / Wakha.
No, you can't. / It's not possible. لا، ما يمكنش ليك. / لا. La, ma yemken-sh lik. / La. `ma yemken-sh` (it's not possible)
Is it allowed...? / Is it permitted...? واش مسموح...؟ Wash mesmu7...? `mesmu7` (allowed)
It's forbidden / Not allowed. ممنوع. Memnu3.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Expressing Ability

Translate to Darija using `qder` (can):

  1. I can swim. (`3um` - to swim) Kanqder n3um.
  2. She can cook tagine very well. (`tiyyeb`) Katqder tiyyeb ttajin mzyan bzzaf.
  3. Could you (m.s.) help me yesterday? (`3awen`) Wash qderti t3awenni l-bare7?
  4. They cannot come tomorrow. Ma ghadi-sh yqdru yjiw ghdda. / Ma kayqdrush yjiw ghdda. (Future or general inability)

Exercise 2: Expressing Possibility

Translate using `yemken`:

  1. Maybe we will go to the beach. (`l-b7er` - beach) Yemken ghadi nmshiw l l-b7er.
  2. It's possible for him to finish the work today. (`salla l-khedma`) Yemken lih ysali l-khedma l-yum.
  3. Maybe she didn't understand. Yemken belli ma fhemtsh.

Exercise 3: Asking for Permission

How would you ask the following in Darija?

  1. Can I open the window (shrjem)? Wash yemken liya n7ell sh-shrjem? (`n7ell` - I open)
  2. Is it allowed to take photos here? (`صور` suwwer - to take photos) Wash mesmu7 nsuwwer hna?
  3. Can you (f.s.) wait a minute, please? (`tsenna dqiqa`) Wash yemken lik ttsennay dqiqa, 3afak?
These modal-like expressions (`qder`, `yemken`) are fundamental for expressing nuances beyond simple statements. Practice them in different tenses too!
