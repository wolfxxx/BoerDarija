B1 Module 8: Derived Verb Forms I (Form II - Causative/Intensive)

Unlock a new layer of Darija by understanding derived verb forms, starting with Form II, which often adds a causative or intensive meaning.

Introduction to Derived Verb Forms (`l-awzan`)

In Arabic and its dialects like Darija, most verbs are derived from a three-consonant root (e.g., K-T-B for "write"). By adding prefixes, suffixes, or changing the vowel patterns and doubling consonants, new verbs with related meanings can be formed. These patterns are called `awzan` (أوزان - measures/patterns).

Understanding these patterns can help you guess the meaning of new verbs and expand your vocabulary significantly. We start with one of the most common: Form II.

Form II: The `fe33el` (فعّــل) Pattern

Form II is typically characterized by the **doubling of the middle root consonant**. It often imparts one of these meanings to the basic Form I verb:

  • Causative: To make someone/something do the action of the Form I verb (e.g., to make understand, to make enter).
  • Intensive: To do the action of the Form I verb with intensity or repetition (e.g., to break into pieces vs. to break).
  • Sometimes it creates a new verb with a related but distinct meaning.

The present tense prefix often becomes `kay-` for "he" (instead of just `ka-y-`).

Examples of Form II Verbs:

Form I Verb (Root/Meaning) Form II Verb (Script/Trans.) Meaning Change Example Sentence (Form II) Audio (Form II)
فهم (fhem) - to understand فهّــم / fhhem Causative: To make understand, to explain الأستاذ كايفهّــم الدرس. (L-ustad kayfhhem d-ders.) - The teacher explains the lesson.
دخل (dkhel) - to enter دخّــل / dekhkhel Causative: To make enter, to bring in, to insert دخّــلت الطموبيل للݣــاراج. (Dekhkhlt t-tomobil l l-garaj.) - I put the car in the garage.
خرج (khrej) - to exit/go out خرّج / kherrej Causative: To make go out, to take out, to fire (from job) خرّجت ستيلو من الصاك. (Kherrejt stilo mn s-sak.) - I took a pen out of the bag.
رجع (rje3) - to return رجّــع / rejje3 Causative: To make return, to give back خاصني نرجّــع ليك كتابك. (Khassni nrejje3 lik ktabek.) - I need to give you back your book.
كبر (kber) - to grow up/become big كبّــر / kebber Causative/Intensive: To make big, to enlarge, to raise (children) كبّــرت الصورة. (Kebbert s-sura.) - I enlarged the picture.
هرس (hres) - to break هرّس / herres Intensive: To smash, break into pieces الولد هرّس الزاجة. (L-weld herres z-zaja.) - The boy smashed the glass.

Conjugating Form II Verbs

Form II verbs generally conjugate like regular verbs in the past, present (`ka-`), and future (`ghadi`), keeping the doubled middle consonant.

Example: `fhhem` (فهّــم - to explain)

  • **Past:** Ana `fhhemt`, Nta `fhhemti`, Huwa `fhhem`, Hiya `fhhemet`, 7na `fhhemna`, Huma `fhhemu`
  • **Present (`ka-`):** Ana `kanfhhem`, Nta `katfhhem`, Huwa `kayfhhem`
  • **Future (`ghadi`):** Ana `ghadi nfhhem`, Nta `ghadi tfhhem`, Huwa `ghadi yfhhem`
Listen for the Shadda: The doubled middle consonant (marked by a "shadda" ّ in Arabic script) is important for pronunciation and distinguishes Form II from Form I.

Identifying Form II Verbs

When you encounter a new verb in Darija:

  • Listen for a doubled middle consonant sound.
  • If you know the Form I meaning, consider if the new verb has a causative ("to make someone do X") or intensive ("to do X a lot/strongly") meaning.
  • Over time and with exposure, you'll start to recognize the patterns.

Not all verbs have a common Form II, and sometimes a Form II verb exists without an obvious Form I counterpart in everyday use.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Meaning Match

Match the Form I verb with its likely Form II meaning.

  1. `wsel` (to arrive)
  2. `khrej` (to go out)
  3. `qra` (to read)
  • A. To make read / to teach
  • B. To make arrive / to deliver
  • C. To make go out / to take out

Answers: 1-B (`wssel`), 2-C (`kherrej`), 3-A (`qerra`)

Exercise 2: Form the Sentence

Use the Form II verb in parentheses to complete the sentence.

  1. الأستاذة كاتفهّــم مزيان. (`fhhem` - Hiya, present) - The teacher explains well.
  2. أنا رجّــعت الكتاب للمكتبة البارح. (`rejje3` - Ana, past) - I returned the book to the library yesterday.
  3. غدا، هو غادي يوصّــل الأمانة. (`wssel` - Huwa, future) - Tomorrow, he will deliver the package. (`l-amana` = package/trust)

Exercise 3: Translate to Darija

  1. My mother taught me how to cook. (to teach - `3llem`, Form II of `3lem` - to know)
    Answer: ماما علّماتني كيفاش نطيب. (Mama 3ellmatni kifash ntiyyeb.)
  2. The company employed many people last year. (to employ/make work - `kheddem`)
    Answer: الشركة خدّمات بزاف د الناس العام اللي فات. (Sh-sharika kheddmat bzzaf d n-nas l-3am lli fat.)
Recognizing Form II verbs is a powerful tool for expanding your vocabulary. Pay attention to that doubled middle consonant!
