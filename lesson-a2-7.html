A2 Module 7: Advanced Adjectives & Descriptions

Expand your descriptive skills with more adjectives for personality and appearance, learn about intensifiers, and practice using multiple adjectives.

Review: Adjective Agreement

Remember from A1 (Module 6), adjectives in Darija generally come after the noun and must agree in gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural).

  • Masculine singular: e.g., `ktab kbir` (big book)
  • Feminine singular (often add `-a`): e.g., `dar kbira` (big house)
  • Plural (often add `-in` or irregular): e.g., `wlad fr7anin` (happy children), `ktub kbar` (big books - irregular adj. plural)

Hair & Eyes: Describing hair and eye color often uses phrases.
  • Hair: `sh3r-u k7el` (his hair is black), `sh3r-ha twil` (her hair is long). (`sh3r` = hair)
  • Eyes: `3in-ih zrqin` (his eyes are blue - plural adj.), `3in-iha kbar` (her eyes are big). (`3in`=eye, `3inin`=eyes)

Using Multiple Adjectives

When using more than one adjective to describe a noun, you usually connect them with `u` (و - and). All adjectives must agree with the noun.

Example: A big and new house (Fem. Sing.)
دار كبيرة و جديدة.
Dar kbira u jdida.

Example: A tall and kind man (Masc. Sing.)
راجل طويل و ضريف.
Rajl twil u drif.

Example: Happy and smart children (Plural)
ولاد فرحانين و ذكيين.
Wlad fr7anin u dkiyin.

Intensifiers: Making Adjectives Stronger or Weaker

These words modify the strength of an adjective.

English Intensifier Darija (Script/Trans.) Example (Darija Transcription) Meaning of Example Audio
Very بزاف / bzzaf Dar kbira bzzaf. A very big house.
A little / A bit شوية / shwiya Huwa twil shwiya. He is a little tall.
Too (much) بزاف / bzzaf (can also mean "too much") Had l-film twil bzzaf. This film is too long.
Really / Truly بصح / bssa7 Hiya dkiya bssa7. She is truly smart.
Placement of `bzzaf` and `shwiya`: These usually come *after* the adjective they modify.

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Choose the Right Form

Select the correct adjective form to complete the sentence.

  1. L-bent (طويل / طويلة) → twila (The girl is tall.)
  2. R-rajl (ضريف / ضريفة) → drif (The man is kind.)
  3. L-ktub (جديد / جداد) → jdad (The books are new.)
  4. Had l-qhwa (سخون / سخونة) → skhuna (This coffee (f.) is hot.) (skhun=hot)

Exercise 2: Describe People/Things

Translate these descriptions into Darija:

  1. A funny and smart boy. Weld mDe77ek u dki.
  2. A very old car (fem.). Tomobil qdima bzzaf.
  3. The shy girls are kind. L-bnat l-7shumiyin drifat. (Note: plural adjective agreement)
  4. This food is a little cold. (food: ماكلة makla (f.); cold: بارد bard (m) / باردة barda (f))
    Had l-makla barda shwiya.
Try describing people you know or things around you using these new adjectives and intensifiers. Pay close attention to gender and number agreement!
