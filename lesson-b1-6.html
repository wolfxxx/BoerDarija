B1 Module 6: More Complex Conditionals (Hypotheticals)

Learn to talk about unreal situations in the present or past ("If I had..., I would...") and express hypothetical outcomes.

Review: Simple Future Conditionals (A2)

In A2 (Module 14), you learned the common structure for real future possibilities:

`Ila` + [Verb in Past Tense], `ghadi` + [Verb]**

Example: إلى مشيت للسوق، غادي نشري الخضرة. (Ila mshit l s-souq, ghadi nshri l-khdra.) - If I go to the market, I will buy vegetables.

Now, we'll explore conditionals that talk about situations that are not real or are contrary to fact.

Present Unreal Conditionals: "If... were/did..., ...would..."

This type of conditional talks about a hypothetical situation in the present that is not true, and its imagined result.

Common Structure: **`kun` + [Verb in Past Tense (for the "if" clause)] , `kun` + [Verb in Past Tense (for the "would" clause)]**

Alternatively: **`kun` + [Verb in Past Tense], `ghadi` + [Verb Stem (for "would" clause - less common for true unreal present)]**

The word `kun` (كون) or `kunt` (كنت - if 'I' am the subject of the 'if' clause) often signals this unreality. The repetition of `kun` in both clauses is common.

English Darija (Script) Transcription Breakdown / Notes Audio
If I had money (now), I would travel. كون كان عندي الفلوس، كون سافرت. Kun kan 3ndi l-flus, kun safert. If I had (past form for unreal present), I would have traveled (past form for would result)
If he were here (now), he would help us. كون كان هنا، كون عاونا. Kun kan hna, kun 3awenna. If he was (past for unreal present), he would have helped us (past for would result)
If I were you (m.s.), I would study. كون كنت ف بلاصتك، كون قريت. Kun kunt f blasstek, kun qrit. `kunt f blasstek` = I were in your place
Using Past Tense for Unreal Present: Notice that, like in English "If I *were* rich...", Darija often uses past tense verb forms in both the "if" clause (`kun kan 3ndi...`) and the "would" clause (`kun safert...`) to indicate that the present situation is hypothetical or contrary to fact.

Past Unreal Conditionals: "If... had..., ...would have..."

This type describes a hypothetical situation in the past that did *not* happen, and its imagined past result.

Structure: **`kun` + [Verb in Past Tense (for the "if" clause)] , `kun rani / rak / rah...` + [Verb in Past Tense (for the "would have" clause)]**

The `rani/rak/rah` part (I am/you are/he is - but here used for hypothetical past result) is common here. It conjugates: `rani` (I would have), `rak` (you s. would have), `rah` (he would have), `raha` (she would have), `rana` (we would have), `rakum` (you pl. would have), `rahum` (they would have).

English Darija (Script) Transcription Breakdown / Notes Audio
If I had studied, I would have passed. كون قريت، كون راني نجحت. Kun qrit, kun rani nje7t. If I studied (past), I would have passed (past)
If she had come earlier, she would have seen him. كون جات بكري، كون راها شافتو. Kun jat bekri, kun raha shafetu. If she came (past), she would have seen him (past)
If they had had money, they would have traveled. كون كان عندهم الفلوس، كون راهُم سافرو. Kun kan 3ndhum l-flus, kun rahum safru. If they had (past), they would have traveled (past)
`Lu kan` (لو كان): For a stronger sense of "If only..." or a more emphasized hypothetical, `lu kan` can be used instead of just `kun` at the beginning of the "if" clause. The rest of the structure often remains similar.
Example: لو كان عرفت، كون جيت. (Lu kan 3reft, kun jit.) - If only I had known, I would have come.

Expressing Wishes/Regrets (Related to Conditionals)

Wishes about the present or past often use similar structures with `kun`.

  • I wish I had money (now): كون كان عندي الفلوس! (Kun kan 3ndi l-flus!) - (Same as present unreal condition).
  • I wish I had studied (regret about the past): كون (غار) قريت! (Kun (ghar) qrit!) - (`ghar` غار adds emphasis like "if only").

Practice Time!

Exercise 1: Present Unreal Conditionals

Complete the sentences. (Assume the result clause also uses `kun` + Past Tense)

  1. If I were a bird (طير - tir), kun tert. (tert - I would fly, from طار - tar)
  2. If he knew (عرف - 3ref) the answer, kun qal lina. (qal lina - he would tell us)
  3. If we had a car (now), kun mshina l l-b7er. (mshina l l-b7er - we would go to the beach)

Exercise 2: Past Unreal Conditionals

Complete the sentences using `kun` + Past Tense in both clauses (add `rani/rak/rah...` in the result clause).

  1. If you (m.s.) had called me (عيّطتي ليا - 3iyyetti liya), kun rani jit. (jit - I would have come)
  2. If she hadn't been sick (ما كانتش مريضة - ma kanetsh mrida), kun raha khdmet. (khdmet - she would have worked)
  3. If they had asked (سوّلو - suwwlu), kun rana 3awennahum. (3awennahum - we would have helped them)

Exercise 3: Translate to Darija

  1. If I had money now, I would buy a new phone.
    Answer: Kun kan 3ndi l-flus daba, kun shrit tilifun jdid.
  2. If you (f.s.) had told me yesterday, I would have helped you.
    Answer: Kun qelti liya l-bare7, kun rani 3awntek.
  3. I wish he were here!
    Answer: Kun kan hna!
Hypothetical conditionals are more advanced but very useful for expressing regrets, wishes, and imagining different scenarios. Focus on recognizing the `kun` marker for unreality.
