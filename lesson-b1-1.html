B1 Module 1: A2 Review & Advanced Past Tense Nuances

Welcome to B1 Intermediate Darija! We'll start by refreshing key A2 concepts and then explore more complex aspects of the past tense, including "hollow" and "weak" verbs.

Quick A2 Review: Building Blocks

Before diving into B1 material, let's ensure our A2 foundation is solid. You should be comfortable with:

  • Regular & common irregular past tenses (A2.1, A2.2)
  • Object pronoun suffixes (A2.3)
  • Future tense with `ghadi` (A2.5)
  • Comparatives & superlatives (A2.8)
  • Relative clauses with `lli` (A2.13)
  • Simple conditionals with `ila` (A2.14)
  • Expressing opinions & suggestions (A2.15)
  • Expanded vocabulary (daily routines, health, travel, etc.)

This module will build directly on your knowledge of past tense formation.

Revisiting Past Tense Conjugation

Recall the general past tense suffixes from A2 Module 1:

  • I: `-t` (e.g., `ktebt` - I wrote)
  • You (s.): `-ti`
  • He: (root, or root + `a`)
  • She: `-at`
  • We: `-na`
  • You (pl.): `-tu`
  • They: `-u` (often with a vowel change in the stem)

Now, we'll look at verb types that have specific changes in the past tense due to their root structure.

"Hollow" Verbs (Verbs with a Middle Weak Letter `waw` or `ya'`)

"Hollow" verbs have a weak middle root consonant, typically و (waw) or ي (ya'), which often disappears or changes to a long vowel in conjugation. `kan` (كان - to be) which you learned in A2 is a prime example.

Example: `qal` (قال - to say) - Past Tense

Pronoun Darija (Script) Transcription Meaning Audio
Ana (I)قلتqeltI said
Nta/Nti (You s.)قلتيqeltiYou said
Huwa (He)قالqalHe said
Hiya (She)قالتqaletShe said
7na (We)قلناqelnaWe said
Ntuma (You pl.)قلتوqeltuYou (pl) said
Huma (They)قالوqaluThey said
Pattern for Hollow Verbs: Notice how the middle weak letter often becomes a short vowel (`e` in `qelt`) when suffixes starting with a consonant are added (`-t`, `-na`). For suffixes starting with a vowel or for Huwa/Huma, the long vowel of the root often remains (`qal`, `qalu`).

Another Hollow Verb: `ba3` (باع - to sell)

  • Ana `be3t` (بعت) - I sold
  • Huwa `ba3` (باع) - He sold
  • Huma `ba3u` (باعو) - They sold

"Weak" Verbs (Verbs with a Final Weak Letter `alif` or `ya'`)

"Weak" verbs end in a weak letter, usually ى (alif maqsura, sounds like 'a') or ي (ya', sounds like 'i'). `msha` (مشى - to go), which you saw in A2, is an example.

Example: `nsa` (نسى - to forget) - Past Tense

Pronoun Darija (Script) Transcription Meaning Audio
AnaنسيتnsitI forgot
Nta/NtiنسيتيnsitiYou forgot
HuwaنسىnsaHe forgot
HiyaنساتnsatShe forgot
7naنسيناnsinaWe forgot
NtumaنسيتوnsituYou (pl) forgot
HumaنساوnsawThey forgot
Pattern for Weak Verbs (ending in -a sound): The final weak letter often changes to `-it` for "Ana", `-iti` for "Nta/Nti", `-at` for "Hiya", `-ina` for "7na", `-itu` for "Ntuma", and `-aw` for "Huma". The "Huwa" form is often just the root.

Using Advanced Past Tense in Storytelling

Now you can make your narratives more interesting by using a wider range of verbs.

Example: البارح، قلت لصاحبي "يلاه نمشيو للقهوة." هو قال "واخا." مشينا و شربنا قهوة. نسيت الفلوس ديالي فالدار، ولكن هو خلص عليا.
L-bare7, qelt l-sa7bi "Yallah nmshiw l l-qhwa." Huwa qal "wakha." Mshina u shrebna qhwa. Nsit l-flus dyali f d-dar, walakin huwa khelles 3liya.
(Yesterday, I said to my friend, "Let's go to the café." He said, "Okay." We went and drank coffee. I forgot my money at home, but he paid for me.)

  • `qelt` - I said (hollow verb)
  • `qal` - he said (hollow verb)
  • `mshina` - we went (weak verb)
  • `shrebna` - we drank (regular verb)
  • `nsit` - I forgot (weak verb)
  • `khelles 3liya` - he paid for me (`khelles` = to pay, `3liya` = for me/on me)

Exercise 1: Conjugate Hollow Verbs

Conjugate the verb `ba3` (باع - to sell) in the past tense for:

  1. Hiya (She): ba3et
  2. 7na (We): be3na
  3. Ntuma (You pl.): be3tu

Exercise 2: Conjugate Weak Verbs

Conjugate the verb `bqa` (بقى - to stay/remain) in the past tense for:

  1. Ana (I): bqit
  2. Huwa (He): bqa
  3. Huma (They): bqaw

Exercise 3: Translate to Darija

Translate these sentences using the appropriate past tense forms:

  1. I said "Hello" to him. Qelt lih "Ssalām".
  2. She forgot her keys. Nsat s-swaret dyalha.
  3. They sold their house last year. Ba3u darhum l-3am lli fat.
  4. We stayed at the hotel for two days. Bqina f l'otel yumayn. (yumayn = two days)
Mastering these "hollow" and "weak" verb patterns in the past tense is a big step in Darija fluency, as they are very common. Listen to the audio examples carefully and practice their forms!
